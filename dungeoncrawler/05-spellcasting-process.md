# Pathfinder 2E: Spellcasting Process

## Overview

Spellcasting in Pathfinder 2E involves invoking magical energy through prepared or spontaneous casting. Understanding spell components, actions, and slots is essential for spellcasters.

## Cast a Spell Activity

Casting a spell is an activity that takes a variable number of actions.

**Common Spell Actions**:
- **[one-action]**: Quick spells (some cantrips)
- **[two-actions]**: Most standard spells
- **[three-actions]**: Powerful or ritual spells
- **[reaction]**: Reactive spells (Shield, Feather Fall)
- **[free-action]**: Rare instant spells

**Visual Manifestations**: Casting creates obvious visual effects that others can see

## Spell Components

Spells require one or more components to cast:

### Material Component (Manipulate Trait)

**Requirements**:
- Physical matter that is consumed or used
- Requires **free hand** to manipulate materials
- Triggers attacks of opportunity (manipulate trait)

**Usage**:
- Common for arcane and primal spells
- Can use spell component pouch
- Can be substituted by focus item (for some classes)

### Somatic Component (Manipulate Trait)

**Requirements**:
- Hand gestures and movements
- Can hold something but need freedom to gesture
- Requires at least one free hand

**Usage**:
- Common across all traditions
- Triggers attacks of opportunity (manipulate trait)
- Can't be used while grabbed or restrained (without special feat)

### Verbal Component (Concentrate Trait)

**Requirements**:
- Spoken words in strong, clear voice
- Must be able to speak
- Has auditory trait

**Usage**:
- Common across all traditions
- Can't be used while silenced
- Can't be used while gagged
- Concentrate trait means can be disrupted

### Focus Component (Manipulate Trait)

**Requirements**:
- Special object that channels magical energy
- Specific to caster class
- Replaces material components

**Examples**:
- **Clerics**: Religious symbol (divine focus)
- **Druids**: Primal focus (holly, mistletoe ,etc.)
- **Wizards**: Staff or bonded item
- **Bards**: Musical instrument (can replace somatic + material + verbal)

## Component Substitutions

Different classes can substitute components:

| Class | Substitution |
|-------|--------------|
| **Bard** | Can use instrument for somatic, material, and verbal components |
| **Cleric** | Divine focus replaces material components |
| **Druid** | Primal focus replaces material components |
| **Sorcerer** | Somatic components can replace material for bloodline tradition spells |
| **Wizard** | Can use staff or bonded item for material components |

**Innate Spells**: Automatically replace material components with somatic components

## Spell Slots

Spellcasters have limited magical energy represented by spell slots.

### Spell Slot System

- **Slots per level**: Spellcasters have different numbers of slots for each spell level
- **Expending slots**: Casting a spell expends a slot of the spell's level
- **Daily recovery**: Regain spell slots during daily preparations
- **Cantrips**: Don't use spell slots (unlimited casting)

**Example**: 5th-level wizard might have:
- Cantrips: Unlimited
- 1st-level: 3 slots
- 2nd-level: 3 slots
- 3rd-level: 2 slots

### Prepared vs Spontaneous Casting

**Prepared Casters** (Cleric, Druid, Wizard):
- Choose specific spells during daily preparations
- Fill each slot with a specific spell
- Can only cast prepared spells
- More flexible with spell selection day-to-day

**Spontaneous Casters** (Bard, Sorcerer):
- Have repertoire of known spells
- Can cast any known spell using any appropriate slot
- More flexible during adventuring day
- Limited total spells known

**Example**:
- **Prepared Wizard**: Prepares Magic Missile in 1st-level slot #1, Magic Missile in slot #2, Mage Armor in slot #3
- **Spontaneous Sorcerer**: Knows Magic Missile and Mage Armor, can cast either using any 1st-level slot

### Cantrips

**Special Features**:
- 0-level spells
- Don't use spell slots
- Can cast **unlimited** times per day
- Automatically heightened to half your level (rounded up)

**Common Cantrips**:
- Detect Magic
- Light
- Shield
- Electric Arc
- Ray of Frost
- Telekinetic Projectile

### Heightening Spells

**Heightening** = Casting a lower-level spell using a higher-level slot

**Effects**:
- Increases spell's power
- Each spell describes heightened effects
- Automatic for some aspects (damage, duration, etc.)

**Example**: 
- *Magic Missile* (1st level): 1d4+1 damage per missile
- *Magic Missile* (heightened to 3rd level): 2 additional missiles

## Spell Durations

### Instantaneous
- Effect happens immediately
- No ongoing duration
- Effects permanent (within reason)

**Examples**: Fireball, Heal, Magic Missile

### Rounds
- Lasts specified number of rounds
- Decreases by 1 at **start of caster's turn**
- Tracked separately for each spell

**Examples**: 
- Bless (1 minute = 10 rounds)
- Haste (1 minute = 10 rounds)

### Sustained
- Requires **Sustain a Spell** action [one-action] each round
- Maximum 10 minutes unless specified
- Ends if you don't sustain
- Concentrate trait

**Sustain a Spell** [one-action]
- Concentrate trait
- Extends spell for 1 more round
- Can only sustain spells cast by you
- Can sustain multiple spells but requires separate actions

**Examples**:
- Flaming Sphere
- Dancing Lights
- Spiritual Weapon

### Until Next Daily Preparations
- Lasts until caster prepares spells again
- Can extend duration by not preparing spell in that slot
- Typically 24 hours

**Examples**:
- Mage Armor
- Longstrider

### Unlimited
- Lasts indefinitely
- Continues until counteracted or dismissed
- Rare

**Examples**: Some rituals and permanent effects

## Spell Disruption

If spell casting is disrupted, the spell fails:

**What Happens**:
- Spell **lost** (doesn't take effect)
- Spell slot **expended**
- Actions **spent** (wasted)
- Material costs **paid** (consumed)

**Common Causes of Disruption**:
- Taking damage while casting (concentrate actions)
- Being moved against your will
- Environmental effects (storm, earthquake)
- Counterspell
- Being knocked unconscious

**Reactions**: Reaction spells cast instantly, so rarely disrupted

## Spell Attack Rolls

Some spells require attack rolls:

**Spell Attack Roll** = d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus + other bonuses

- Compare to target's **AC**
- Can critically hit (beat AC by 10+)
- Subject to Multiple Attack Penalty if casting multiple spell attacks

**Examples**:
- Ray of Frost (cantrip)
- Shocking Grasp
- Telekinetic Projectile

## Spell Saving Throws

Many spells allow targets to resist:

**Spell DC** = 10 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus + other bonuses

**Target Rolls**: Fortitude, Reflex, or Will save
- Result determines effectiveness
- Uses standard degrees of success

**Basic Saves**: Special wording that applies damage scaling:
- **Critical Success**: No damage
- **Success**: Half damage
- **Failure**: Full damage
- **Critical Failure**: Double damage

**Examples**:
- Fireball (basic Reflex save)
- Fear (Will save)
- Slow (Fortitude save)

## Spell Ranges and Areas

### Ranges

- **Touch**: Must touch target (melee spell attack)
- **30 feet**: Short range spell
- **60 feet**: Medium range spell (common)
- **120+ feet**: Long range spell
- **500 feet**: Very long range
- **Line of sight**: Can see target
- **Unlimited**: Special circumstances

### Areas

**Burst**: Radiates from point, affects all in radius
- Example: Fireball (20-foot burst)

**Cone**: Quarter-circle spreading from caster
- Example: Burning Hands (15-foot cone)

**Emanation**: Radiates from caster
- Example: Bless (15-foot emanation)

**Line**: Straight line from caster
- Example: Lightning Bolt (60-foot line)

## Spell Traditions

Four magical traditions with different focuses:

### Arcane (Intelligence)
- **Source**: Study and logic
- **Classes**: Wizard
- **Focus**: Alteration, knowledge, mental manipulation
- **Typical Spells**: Fireball, Invisibility, Fly

### Divine (Wisdom)
- **Source**: Faith and devotion
- **Classes**: Cleric, Champion
- **Focus**: Healing, protection, alignment-based
- **Typical Spells**: Heal, Bless, Divine Lance

### Occult (Charisma or Intelligence)
- **Source**: Hidden mysteries
- **Classes**: Bard
- **Focus**: Mental, emotion, fate, prediction
- **Typical Spells**: Charm, Hideous Laughter, Invisibility

### Primal (Wisdom)
- **Source**: Nature and instinct
- **Classes**: Druid, Ranger
- **Focus**: Nature, elements, animals, plants
- **Typical Spells**: Heal, Summon Animal, Flaming Sphere

## Concentration

Many spells have the **Concentrate** trait:

**Rules**:
- Requires mental focus
- Can be disrupted by damage
- Can't concentrate while unconscious
- Can maintain multiple concentrate effects simultaneously

**Doesn't Require Sustaining**:
- Many concentrate spells don't need Sustain action
- They just require focus while being cast

## Metamagic

Some classes can modify spells with metamagic:

**How It Works**:
- Use metamagic action (usually [one-action] or [free-action])
- Immediately follow with Cast a Spell
- Can't use other actions/reactions between them
- Spell gains benefits described by metamagic

**Example Metamagic** (Sorcerer):
- **Reach Spell** [one-action]: Increase spell range by 30 feet
- **Widen Spell** [one-action]: Increase burst/emanation/cone by 5 feet

## Spell Descriptions

Each spell lists:
- **Level**: Spell level (cantrip, 1-10)
- **Traditions**: Which traditions can cast it
- **Cast**: Actions required
- **Components**: Material, somatic, verbal, etc.
- **Range/Area**: How far and what it affects
- **Targets**: What can be targeted
- **Duration**: How long it lasts
- **Saving Throw**: If targets can resist
- **Description**: What the spell does
- **Heightened**: Effects when cast at higher level

## Example Spellcasting Scenarios

### Example 1: Simple Offensive Spell

**Ezren** (3rd-level wizard) casts Ray of Frost
- **Action**: [two-actions] Cast a Spell
- **Components**: Somatic, Verbal (no material)
- **Spell Attack**: d20 + 7 (Int +4, Trained +3) = 22
- **Target AC**: 17
- **Result**: Hit! Roll 2d4+4 cold damage

### Example 2: Area Spell with Save

**Seoni** (5th-level sorcerer) casts Fireball
- **Action**: [two-actions] Cast a Spell
- **Components**: Somatic, Verbal (innate spell, no material)
- **Range**: 500 feet
- **Area**: 20-foot burst
- **Targets**: 4 goblins in area
- **Save**: Basic Reflex vs DC 21
- **Goblin Results**:
  - Goblin 1: Critical Success (no damage)
  - Goblin 2: Success (half damage)
  - Goblin 3: Failure (full damage)
  - Goblin 4: Critical Failure (double damage)

### Example 3: Sustained Spell

**Kyra** (4th-level cleric) casts Spiritual Weapon
- **Action**: [two-actions] Cast a Spell (round 1)
- **Duration**: Sustained up to 1 minute
- **Round 1**: Spell appears, can make Strike [one-action]
- **Round 2**: [one-action] Sustain, [one-action] Strike weapon again
- **Round 3**: [one-action] Sustain, uses other 2 actions for something else
- **Round 4**: Doesn't sustain, spell ends

### Example 4: Reaction Spell

**Wizard** preparing to cast:
- Enemy shoots arrow at wizard
- **[reaction] Cast Shield**: AC increases from 13 to 18
- Arrow misses
- Shield expires at start of wizard's next turn

## Tips for Spellcasting

1. **Manage slots**: Don't waste high-level slots on easy encounters
2. **Use cantrips**: Free damage/utility between leveled spells
3. **Positioning**: Stay out of melee range to avoid disruption
4. **Know your spells**: Read spell descriptions carefully
5. **Heighten wisely**: Some spells scale better than others
6. **Buff before combat**: Cast long-duration spells before fights
7. **Save reactions**: Shield and Counterspell can be lifesavers
8. **Learn enemy saves**: Target weak saves (low AC → spell attacks, low Reflex → Reflex saves)
