# APG Chapter 3: Archetypes — Requirements Extraction

Source: PF2E Advanced Players Guide, Chapter 3 (lines 21632–29054)
Status: Complete

## Archetype System Rules (General)

- REQ: Archetype feats are multi-class or specialization options obtained by selecting a Dedication feat (minimum L2) as a class feat
- REQ: Each archetype requires selecting the Dedication feat first before any other archetype feats
- REQ: Cannot select a second Dedication feat from the same archetype until 2 other feats from that archetype are selected (the "2-before-another-dedication" rule)
- REQ: Multiclass spellcasting archetypes follow basic/expert/master spellcasting progression patterns

---

## SECTION: Acrobat

### Paragraph — Acrobat Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Acrobatics. Become expert in Acrobatics. Upgrade to master at L7, legendary at L15. Critical success on Tumble Through: ignore enemy space as difficult terrain.

- REQ: Acrobat Dedication grants expert Acrobatics proficiency immediately
- REQ: Acrobatics auto-scales: master at L7, legendary at L15 via this archetype
- REQ: Critical Tumble Through success ignores that enemy's space as difficult terrain

## SECTION: Archaeologist

### Paragraph — Archaeologist Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Perception, Society, and Thievery. Become expert in Society and Thievery. +1 circumstance bonus to Recall Knowledge about ancient history, peoples, and cultures.

- REQ: Grants expert proficiency in both Society and Thievery
- REQ: +1 circumstance bonus to Recall Knowledge about ancient/historical subjects

## SECTION: Archer

### Paragraph — Archer Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: none specified. Become trained in all simple and martial bow weapons. Scale to expert/master/legendary as class features grant those proficiencies. If expert with a bow, gain critical specialization with that bow.

- REQ: Archer Dedication grants training in all simple and martial weapons in the bow group
- REQ: Bow proficiency scales with class weapon proficiency advances
- REQ: Expert in a bow = gain critical specialization for that bow

## SECTION: Assassin

### Paragraph — Assassin Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Crafting, Deception, and Stealth + Alchemical Crafting feat. Grants Mark for Death (3-action activity): designate one creature as your mark (1 mark at a time). While marked: +2 circumstance bonus to Seek/Feint checks against them; agile/finesse weapons gain backstabber and deadly d6 traits against the mark.

- REQ: Assassin Dedication grants Mark for Death 3-action activity
- REQ: Only one mark active at a time; previous mark ends when new one is designated
- REQ: Mark grants +2 circumstance bonus to Perception checks to Seek and Deception checks to Feint vs. mark
- REQ: Agile and finesse weapons/unarmed attacks gain backstabber + deadly d6 traits vs. mark
- REQ: If the weapon already has deadly trait, increase the die size by one step instead of adding deadly d6

## SECTION: Bastion

### Paragraph — Bastion Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: Shield Block feat. Gain Reactive Shield fighter feat. Counts as having Reactive Shield for prerequisites.

- REQ: Bastion Dedication grants the Reactive Shield fighter feat
- REQ: Satisfies prerequisites that require Reactive Shield

## SECTION: Beastmaster

### Paragraph — Beastmaster Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Nature. Gain a young animal companion. If already have one, this may grant a second companion. Multi-companion rule requires Call Companion action to switch which companion is "active." Focus spells from beastmaster feats use Charisma; trained in primal spell attack/DC when first beastmaster focus spell is gained.

- REQ: Beastmaster grants a young animal companion
- REQ: Can stack with existing animal companion to have two (unusual — most classes cap at one)
- REQ: When owning multiple companions, gain Call Companion action (1-action exploration) to switch active companion
- REQ: Beastmaster focus spells use Charisma as spellcasting ability; primal tradition
- REQ: Focus pool created at 1 Focus Point when first focus spell is gained; Refocus by tending to companion

## SECTION: Blessed One

### Paragraph — Blessed One Dedication (L2)

> No prerequisites listed (available to any). Gain lay on hands devotion spell (divine). Focus pool of 1 Focus Point. Refocus by meditating. Does not require specific deity — any deity capable of granting positive divine font can grant this blessing.

- REQ: Blessed One Dedication grants lay on hands devotion spell (divine spell)
- REQ: Creates focus pool of 1 Focus Point; Refocus = meditation (10 min)
- REQ: Available to any character, not restricted to divine casters or specific deities

## SECTION: Bounty Hunter

### Paragraph — Bounty Hunter Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Survival. Gain Hunt Prey action (same as ranger's). Can only designate prey that has been identified in some way (seen, heard about, bounty poster, etc.). +2 circumstance bonus to Gather Information about the prey. If already have Hunt Prey, become expert in Survival instead and gain the bonus.

- REQ: Bounty Hunter Dedication grants Hunt Prey action (Core Rulebook ranger feature)
- REQ: Hunt Prey can only target creatures the hunter has knowledge of (not unknown creatures)
- REQ: +2 circumstance bonus to Gather Information checks about designated prey
- REQ: If already have Hunt Prey (e.g., from Ranger class), gain expert Survival instead

## SECTION: Cavalier

### Paragraph — Cavalier Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Nature or Society. Gain a young mount animal companion (must be at least one size larger than you, or Medium if animal is normally Small). Mount must have the mount special ability. Unusual dedication rule: restriction on taking a second dedication is waived if you pledge to an organization associated with that other archetype.

- REQ: Cavalier Dedication grants a young animal companion that functions as a mount
- REQ: Mount must be at least one size larger than the character; Small-start animals can begin at Medium
- REQ: Mount animal must have the mount special ability
- REQ: Second dedication restriction is waived when pledging to an aligned organization

## SECTION: Celebrity

### Paragraph — Celebrity Dedication (L2)

> Gain Upstage reaction (reaction; trigger: foe fails a skill check; attempt same skill check; critical success = +1 status bonus to all rolls until end of next turn; success = bonus only if foe failed). When Earning Income from a task higher level than your own, gain +1 circumstance bonus to the check.

- REQ: Celebrity Dedication grants Upstage reaction
- REQ: Upstage: when foe fails a skill check, attempt same skill; crit success = +1 status bonus to attack/Perception/saves/skills until end of next turn; success = bonus only if foe failed
- REQ: +1 circumstance bonus to Earn Income checks when task level exceeds character level

### Paragraph — Dandy Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Diplomacy. Gain Influence Rumor downtime activity (Diplomacy skill; at least 1 day; DC varies by community size). Become trained in Deception and Society (or expert if already trained).

- REQ: Dandy Dedication grants Influence Rumor downtime activity using Diplomacy
- REQ: Influence Rumor DC scales by community size (DC 15 village, 20 town, 30 city, 40 metropolis as minimums)
- REQ: Grants trained in Deception and Society; upgrade to expert if already trained

## SECTION: Dragon Disciple

### Paragraph — Dragon Disciple Dedication (L2)

> Uncommon. Access: kobold with dragonscaled/spellscaled heritage, dragon instinct barbarian, or draconic bloodline sorcerer. Choose one dragon color type: gain resistance = half level to associated damage type. +1 circumstance bonus to saves vs. sleep and paralysis effects.

- REQ: Dragon Disciple is Uncommon; restricted access per prerequisites
- REQ: Choose dragon color at dedication: determines energy resistance type
- REQ: Dragon colors: Black/Copper (acid), Blue/Bronze (electricity), Brass/Gold/Red (fire), Green (poison), Silver/White (cold)
- REQ: Energy resistance = half character level (scales automatically)
- REQ: +1 circumstance bonus to saves vs. sleep and paralysis effects

## SECTION: Duelist

### Paragraph — Duelist Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in light armor and simple weapons. Gain Quick Draw ranger feat (draw + attack in 1 action). Special: can swap any duelist archetype feats (except Dedication and Student of the Dueling Arts) each day during daily preparations. Can enter stances from feats you don't have by spending +1 additional action; must still meet prerequisites.

- REQ: Duelist Dedication grants Quick Draw feat
- REQ: Duelist can retrain archetype feats freely during daily preparations (unique flexibility mechanic)
- REQ: Can enter duelist stances from feats not possessed by adding +1 action cost (typically 2 actions total)
- REQ: Must still meet feat prerequisites to use borrowed stance

## SECTION: Eldritch Archer

### Paragraph — Eldritch Archer Dedication (L6)

> Prerequisites: expert in at least one type of bow. If no spellcasting: gain spontaneous spellcasting (1 attack-roll cantrip of choice from any tradition; trained in spell attack/DC; Cha key ability). If already have spellcasting: gain 1 additional cantrip of that tradition. Gain Eldritch Shot (3-action activity): Cast a 1- or 2-action spell requiring a spell attack roll, imbue it into a bow, make a Strike; result applies to both Strike and spell simultaneously; counts as two attacks for MAP but both are resolved before penalty is applied.

- REQ: Eldritch Archer Dedication requires L6 (not L2 like most archetypes)
- REQ: Grants spontaneous spellcasting if not already a caster (1 cantrip, any tradition, Cha key ability)
- REQ: Casters gain 1 extra cantrip from their existing tradition
- REQ: Grants Eldritch Shot: 3-action activity using bow + spell simultaneously
- REQ: Eldritch Shot counts as 2 attacks for MAP; both attack and spell use the same die roll result
- REQ: MAP penalty from both applies after the double-attack is resolved (not before the first)

- REQ: With spectators at combat start, may roll Performance for initiative

## SECTION: Herbalist

### Paragraph — Herbalist Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Nature, Natural Medicine feat. Gain basic alchemy benefits restricted to herbal items (alchemical items with healing trait + antidote + antiplague). Reagents per day = level (or half level if not prepared in wilderness). Advanced alchemy level = 1. Become expert in Nature; may use Nature instead of Crafting to Craft herbal items.

- REQ: Herbalist Dedication grants restricted alchemy (herbal items only)
- REQ: Reagent batch count = character level (or half level if daily prep not done in wilderness)
- REQ: Advanced alchemy level for herbs = 1 and does not auto-scale
- REQ: Grants expert Nature; Nature skill can replace Crafting for herbal item crafting

### Paragraph — Loremaster Dedication (L2)

If legendary in a Decipher Writing skill, Loremaster Lore becomes expert. Bards with Bardic Lore gain +1 circumstance bonus to Bardic Lore checks.

- REQ: Loremaster Dedication grants Loremaster Lore (trained): universal Recall Knowledge skill

## SECTION: Marshal

### Paragraph — Marshal Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in martial weapons and either Diplomacy or Intimidation. Become trained/expert in chosen skill. Gain a marshal's aura (10-foot emanation; emotion/mental/visual traits; +1 status bonus to saves vs. fear for all in aura including self).

- REQ: Marshal Dedication creates a persistent 10-foot marshal's aura
- REQ: Aura has emotion, mental, and visual traits
- REQ: Aura grants +1 status bonus to fear saves for all allies and self within 10 feet

### Paragraph — Dread/Inspiring Marshal Stances (L4)

> **Dread Marshal Stance** (1 action, open, stance; Intimidation): On success, aura expands to 20 feet and grants status bonus to damage = number of weapon damage dice on primary weapon. Critical hits in aura also inflict Frightened 1 on the target. Failure ends the attempt.
> **Inspiring Marshal Stance** (1 action, open, stance; Diplomacy): On success, aura expands to 20 feet and grants +1 status bonus to attack rolls and saves vs. mental effects.

- REQ: Dread Marshal Stance requires trained in Intimidation; rolls Intimidation vs. standard DC for level
- REQ: Dread Stance success: aura expands to 20 feet; status damage bonus = weapon damage dice count; critical hits in aura inflict Frightened 1
- REQ: Inspiring Marshal Stance requires trained in Diplomacy; rolls Diplomacy vs. standard DC for level
- REQ: Inspiring Stance success: aura expands to 20 feet; +1 status to attack rolls and saves vs. mental effects

## SECTION: Medic

### Paragraph — Medic Dedication (L2)

> Prerequisites: trained in Medicine, Battle Medicine feat. Become expert in Medicine. Successful Battle Medicine or Treat Wounds now heals 5 extra HP at DC 20, 10 extra HP at DC 30, 15 extra HP at DC 40. Once per day, can use Battle Medicine on a temporarily immune creature (once per hour if master in Medicine).

- REQ: Medic Dedication grants expert Medicine
- REQ: Battle Medicine and Treat Wounds success bonuses: +5 HP at DC 20, +10 HP at DC 30, +15 HP at DC 40
- REQ: Can bypass temporary Battle Medicine immunity once per day (once per hour at master Medicine)

- REQ: Pirate Dedication grants Sailing Lore (trained or expert)
- REQ: Ignore difficult terrain caused by ship/vehicle movement
- REQ: Gain Boarding Assault (2-action flourish): move + bonus Strike when boarding/disembarking

Gain 1 more uncommon ritual at L8, L12, L16, L20. Can cast these as primary caster only (cannot teach them to others or allow alternate primary caster).

- REQ: Ritualist Dedication requires L4 (not the usual L2)
- REQ: Ritualist is Uncommon access
- REQ: +2 circumstance bonus to all primary ritual casting checks
- REQ: Grants 2 uncommon rituals at selection (max level = half character level)
- REQ: Learn 1 additional uncommon ritual at L8, 12, 16, 20
- REQ: Can only cast learned rituals as primary caster; cannot teach or share primary caster role

## SECTION: Talisman Dabbler

### Paragraph — Talisman Dabbler Dedication (L2)

> Know formulas for all common talismans ≤ your level (memorized, no formula book needed). Each daily prep: create 2 temporary talismans (item level ≤ half your level). Temporary talismans lose magic at next daily prep if unused. When Affixing a Talisman: can affix or remove up to 4 talismans in one 10-minute span.

- REQ: Talisman Dabbler grants all common talisman formulas ≤ character level (memorized)
- REQ: Create 2 free temporary talismans per day (item level ≤ half character level)
- REQ: Unused temporary talismans expire at next daily prep
- REQ: Affixing Talismans: can do up to 4 affixations/removals per 10-minute activity

## SECTION: Vigilante

### Paragraph — Vigilante Dedication (L2)

> Uncommon. Prerequisites: trained in Deception. Grants dual identity (social identity + vigilante identity). Each identity has its own name, alignment, and capabilities. Identities can be at most 1 alignment step apart. Changing identity takes 1 minute (out of sight). Deception DC to detect alternate identity = 20 + proficiency modifier. Effects that detect by identity (e.g., detect alignment) only apply to currently active identity.

- REQ: Vigilante is Uncommon access
- REQ: Character has two distinct identities: social and vigilante
- REQ: Identities may differ in alignment by at most 1 step
- REQ: Switching identities takes 1 minute and must be done out of sight
- REQ: Discovery attempt requires Seek vs. Deception DC (20 + proficiency modifier)
- REQ: Only current identity is detectable by alignment/identity-detection magic
- REQ: Feats tagged "social" only usable in social identity; class feats tagged "vigilante" only usable in vigilante identity
- REQ: Using vigilante abilities in social identity risks exposure; using social abilities in vigilante identity is generally fine
