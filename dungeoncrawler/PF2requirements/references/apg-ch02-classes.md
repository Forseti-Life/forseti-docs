## SECTION: Investigator

### Paragraph — Investigator Core Stats

> HP 8 + Con; Key Ability: Intelligence; Perception: Expert; Saves: Expert Reflex, Expert Will, Trained Fortitude; Weapons: Simple, Martial (ranged), Unarmed; Armor: Light; Class DC: Trained

- REQ: Investigator class has 8 HP + Con per level
- REQ: Investigator key ability is Intelligence; all attack-relevant skill substitutions use Int
- REQ: Investigator starts with Expert Perception, Expert Reflex, Expert Will, Trained Fortitude
- REQ: Investigator is trained in light armor and unarmored defense

### Paragraph — Investigator Skills

> Trained in Society, trained in one or more skills based on methodology, trained in additional skills equal to 4 + Intelligence modifier

- REQ: Investigator gains Society as a class skill
- REQ: Investigator methodology choice grants one additional trained skill
- REQ: Total skill count = 4 + Int modifier (plus Society and methodology skill)

### Paragraph — Devise a Stratagem

> 1 Action, Fortune trait, 1/round. Before attacking a foe you're pursuing a lead on or any foe (if using empiricism), pre-roll a d20 and use the result for your next Strike against that target this turn. If using an agile, finesse, ranged, or sap weapon or unarmed attack, you may use Intelligence instead of Strength or Dexterity for attack rolls. Free action version available if the target is a current active lead.

- REQ: Implement Devise a Stratagem as a 1-action activity with the Fortune trait
- REQ: Pre-rolled d20 result must be stored and applied to the next qualifying Strike the same turn
- REQ: Can only be used once per round (frequency: 1/round)
- REQ: If the target is an active lead, the action costs a Free Action instead of 1 Action
- REQ: Qualifying weapons: agile, finesse, ranged, or sap; unarmed attacks that are agile or finesse
- REQ: When using qualifying weapon, substitute Intelligence modifier for Strength/Dexterity on attack rolls

### Paragraph — Pursue a Lead

> 1 Minute, Exploration. Designate a creature, object, or location as a lead. You gain a +1 circumstance bonus to checks to investigate the lead (specifically Perception, relevant skill checks). Maximum 2 active leads at a time; designating a 3rd lead ends the oldest active lead.

- REQ: Pursue a Lead is a 1-minute exploration activity
- REQ: Grants +1 circumstance bonus to investigating checks against the designated lead target
- REQ: Maximum of 2 active leads may be maintained simultaneously
- REQ: Designating a 3rd lead automatically terminates the oldest existing lead

### Paragraph — Clue In

> Reaction, frequency 1/10 minutes. Trigger: You would succeed or critically succeed at a check to investigate. Effect: Share the circumstance bonus from Pursue a Lead with an ally within 30 feet who could benefit from the information.

- REQ: Clue In is a reaction with frequency 1/10 minutes
- REQ: Triggers on successful investigative checks
- REQ: Shares the circumstance bonus from Pursue a Lead with one ally within 30 feet

### Paragraph — Strategic Strike (Precision Damage)

> When you use Devise a Stratagem and your Strike hits, you deal additional precision damage based on level. +1d6 at 1st, +2d6 at 5th, +3d6 at 9th, +4d6 at 13th, +5d6 at 17th.

- REQ: Strategic Strike adds precision damage to attacks preceded by Devise a Stratagem
- REQ: Damage progression: 1d6 (L1), 2d6 (L5), 3d6 (L9), 4d6 (L13), 5d6 (L17)
- REQ: Damage type is precision (not typed as a specific damage type)

### Paragraph — Methodology: Alchemical Sciences

> Investigator chooses this to gain proficiency in Crafting and the Alchemical Crafting feat. Gains a formula book with common alchemical items. Each day during preparations, creates a number of versatile vials equal to Intelligence modifier (minimum 0). Gains Quick Tincture: 1-action activity to expend a versatile vial and create an infused alchemical item worth the vial's level or lower.

- REQ: Alchemical Sciences methodology grants Crafting proficiency and Alchemical Crafting feat
- REQ: Investigator gains a formula book tracking known alchemical formulas
- REQ: Daily preparations produce a number of free versatile vials equal to Int modifier
- REQ: Quick Tincture is a 1-action activity consuming one versatile vial to produce an alchemical item

### Paragraph — Methodology: Empiricism

> Investigator uses senses and scientific rigor. Gains proficiency in one Intelligence-based skill and the That's Odd feat. Gains Expeditious Inspection: free action usable 1/10 minutes to instantly Recall Knowledge, Seek, or Sense Motive against a target. Devise a Stratagem can target any creature, not just active leads.

- REQ: Empiricism grants proficiency in one Int-based skill and the That's Odd feat
- REQ: Expeditious Inspection is a free action, frequency 1/10 minutes, allowing instant Recall Knowledge, Seek, or Sense Motive
- REQ: Empiricism removes the lead requirement for Devise a Stratagem; can target any creature

### Paragraph — Methodology: Forensic Medicine

> Investigator studies trauma and medicine. Gains proficiency in Medicine and the Battle Medicine feat plus Forensic Acumen. When using Battle Medicine, the target heals additional HP equal to investigator level. The target's immunity to Battle Medicine lasts only 1 hour (not 1 day).

- REQ: Forensic Medicine grants Medicine proficiency, Battle Medicine feat, and Forensic Acumen feat
- REQ: Battle Medicine healing bonus = investigator level (added to normal Battle Medicine healing)
- REQ: Forensic Medicine reduces Battle Medicine immunity duration from 1 day to 1 hour

### Paragraph — Methodology: Interrogation

> Investigator uses social pressure. Gains proficiency in Diplomacy and the No Cause for Alarm feat. Can Pursue a Lead during social conversations (not just exploration). Gains Pointed Question: 1-action activity to point out inconsistencies in a target's story, making the target flat-footed or revealing a lie.

- REQ: Interrogation methodology grants Diplomacy proficiency and No Cause for Alarm feat
- REQ: Allows Pursue a Lead to be used in social encounters/conversation mode
- REQ: Pointed Question is a 1-action Intimidation/Deception-based activity to expose inconsistency

## SECTION: Oracle

### Paragraph — Oracle Core Stats

> HP 8 + Con; Key Ability: Charisma; Perception: Trained; Saves: Expert Will, Trained Fortitude, Trained Reflex; Weapons: Simple, Unarmed; Armor: Light; Spellcasting: Divine, Spontaneous

- REQ: Oracle class has 8 HP + Con per level
- REQ: Oracle key ability is Charisma; spell DC and attack use Cha modifier
- REQ: Oracle saves: Expert Will, Trained Fortitude, Trained Reflex
- REQ: Oracle uses divine spell list with spontaneous (repertoire-based) spellcasting
- REQ: All material components are replaced by somatic components for oracle spellcasting

### Paragraph — Oracle Spell Repertoire

> Starts with 2 first-level spells in repertoire and 5 cantrips. Cantrips are auto-heightened to half oracle level rounded up. Spells per day table mirrors sorcerer (same progression).

- REQ: Oracle begins with 5 cantrips and 2 first-level spells in repertoire
- REQ: Cantrips auto-heighten to half class level rounded up (same as other spontaneous casters)
- REQ: Oracle spells per day follow the same progression as sorcerer (see TABLE 2-3)

### Paragraph — Oracle Signature Spells

> At 3rd level, for each spell level you can cast, designate one spell as a signature spell. Signature spells can be freely heightened without learning separate heightened versions.

- REQ: Oracle gains Signature Spells at 3rd level
- REQ: One spell per accessible spell level may be designated as a signature spell
- REQ: Signature spells can be cast at any available spell level without learning separate versions

### Paragraph — Oracle Mystery System

> Each oracle selects a mystery (Ancestors, Battle, Bones, Cosmos, Flames, Life, Lore, Tempest). The mystery determines: domain spells, initial/advanced/greater revelation spells, and the shape of the oracle's curse.

- REQ: Oracle must select one mystery at character creation
- REQ: Mystery choices: Ancestors, Battle, Bones, Cosmos, Flames, Life, Lore, Tempest
- REQ: Each mystery grants unique domain spell options and revelation spells
- REQ: Each mystery defines the unique oracular curse progression for that oracle

### Paragraph — Revelation Spells (Focus Spells)

> Oracles learn revelation spells as focus spells. All revelation spells have the cursebound trait, meaning they increase the oracle's curse severity when cast. Focus pool starts at 2 Focus Points. Refocus = 10 minutes.

- REQ: Revelation spells are focus spells tagged with the cursebound trait
- REQ: Casting any cursebound spell advances the oracle's curse by one stage
- REQ: Oracle focus pool starts at 2 Focus Points (not 1 like most classes)
- REQ: Refocus takes 10 minutes and restores 1 Focus Point
- REQ: Oracle cannot cast cursebound spells if they do not have an active oracular curse (i.e., have not cast any revelation spell yet that day)

### Paragraph — Initial Revelation Spells

> At 1st level, oracle learns two revelation spells: (1) the initial revelation spell from their mystery, (2) one initial domain spell from a domain associated with their mystery (chosen by player). The domain spell gains the cursebound trait when oracle casts it.

- REQ: Oracle learns exactly 2 revelation spells at 1st level
- REQ: First spell = mystery's initial revelation spell (fixed per mystery)
- REQ: Second spell = player's choice from domains associated with the mystery; gains cursebound trait
- REQ: Both revelation spells cost 1 Focus Point to cast and advance the curse

### Paragraph — Oracular Curse Progression

> The curse has 4 stages: basic (constant, no activation), minor (first revelation spell cast), moderate (second revelation spell cast), major (higher levels unlock), extreme (even higher levels unlock). Minor curse persists until 8-hour rest. Refocusing while at moderate+ reduces curse to minor and restores 1 Focus Point. Casting a revelation spell while at moderate causes "overwhelmed" state — oracle cannot cast any revelation spells until next daily preparations.

- REQ: Implement oracular curse as a 4-stage progression: basic → minor → moderate → (major → extreme at higher levels)
- REQ: Basic effect is passive/constant from character creation
- REQ: Casting any revelation spell when curse is at basic advances to minor
- REQ: Casting any revelation spell when curse is at minor advances to moderate
- REQ: Casting any revelation spell when curse is at moderate triggers "overwhelmed" condition
- REQ: Overwhelmed state prevents casting or sustaining any revelation spells until next daily prep
- REQ: Refocusing while at moderate or higher reduces curse to minor (in addition to restoring 1 Focus Point)
- REQ: Resting 8 hours + daily preparations returns curse to basic state
- REQ: Curse has the curse, divine, and necromancy traits
- REQ: Effects of oracular curse cannot be removed, mitigated, or suppressed by spells or items (e.g., remove curse does not work)
- REQ: At higher levels (GM/system unlocks), major and extreme curse stages become accessible, allowing more revelation spell casting before overwhelmed

## SECTION: Swashbuckler

### Paragraph — Swashbuckler Core Stats

> HP 10 + Con; Key Ability: Dexterity; Perception: Expert; Saves: Trained Fortitude, Expert Reflex, Expert Will; Weapons: Simple, Martial, Unarmed; Armor: Light; Class DC: Trained

- REQ: Swashbuckler class has 10 HP + Con per level
- REQ: Swashbuckler key ability is Dexterity
- REQ: Saves: Trained Fortitude (upgrades at L3), Expert Reflex, Expert Will
- REQ: Trained in simple and martial weapons, light armor, unarmored defense
- REQ: Swashbuckler class DC is tracked separately and starts Trained

### Paragraph — Panache System

> Swashbuckler earns panache by succeeding at style-defined skill checks (see Styles below). While in panache: +5-foot status bonus to all Speeds; +1 circumstance bonus to panache-triggering checks; eligible to use Finisher actions; precise strike deals extra damage. Panache is lost after using a Finisher. Panache ends when encounter ends.

- REQ: Panache is a binary state (in/out), persists until encounter ends or finisher used
- REQ: Panache grants +5-foot status bonus to all movement speeds
- REQ: Panache grants +1 circumstance bonus to checks that would earn panache per style
- REQ: Panache enables use of Finisher actions
- REQ: Panache is earned by succeeding at the style's associated skill check (see Swashbuckler Styles)
- REQ: GM can award panache for particularly daring non-standard actions vs. Very Hard DC
- REQ: Panache is lost immediately upon performing a Finisher

### Paragraph — Swashbuckler Styles

> Style chosen at 1st level. Determines the additional trained skill and how panache is earned.
> - Battledancer: Performance; panache by succeeding on Performance vs. foe's Will DC (Fascinating Performance feat included)
> - Braggart: Intimidation; panache by successfully Demoralizing a foe
> - Fencer: Deception; panache by successfully Feinting or Creating a Diversion
> - Gymnast: Athletics; panache by successfully Grappling, Shoving, or Tripping a foe
> - Wit: Diplomacy + Bon Mot feat; panache by succeeding at Bon Mot against a foe

- REQ: Swashbuckler must select one style at 1st level; style cannot be changed later
- REQ: Each style grants Trained proficiency in its associated skill
- REQ: Battledancer: grants Fascinating Performance feat; panache earned via Performance vs. foe's Will DC
- REQ: Braggart: panache earned via successful Demoralize
- REQ: Fencer: panache earned via successful Feint or Create a Diversion
- REQ: Gymnast: panache earned via successful Grapple, Shove, or Trip
- REQ: Wit: grants Bon Mot skill feat; panache earned via successful Bon Mot

### Paragraph — Precise Strike

> While you have panache, Strikes with agile or finesse melee weapons/unarmed attacks deal +2 precision damage on normal strikes. If the Strike is part of a Finisher, the bonus becomes 2d6 precision damage instead. Scales with level: 1d6 base on non-finisher strikes becomes 1d6/2d6/3d6/4d6/5d6/6d6 at L1/5/9/13/17 (finisher dice), and +2/+3/+4/+5/+6 flat on non-finisher strikes.

- REQ: Precise Strike requires panache and qualifying weapon (agile or finesse melee, or agile/finesse unarmed)
- REQ: Non-finisher Strike bonus: flat precision damage (+2 at L1, +3 at L5, +4 at L9, +5 at L13, +6 at L17)
- REQ: Finisher Strike bonus: precision dice (2d6 at L1, 3d6 at L5, 4d6 at L9, 5d6 at L13, 6d6 at L17)

### Paragraph — Finisher Trait Rules

> Finishers are special actions usable only when you have panache. Immediately upon performing a Finisher (win or lose), you lose panache. After using a Finisher, you cannot use any further actions with the Attack trait that turn. Some Finishers deal partial damage on failure.

- REQ: Finisher actions require panache as a prerequisite
- REQ: Panache is consumed immediately when a Finisher is performed (before outcome resolves)
- REQ: No additional attack-trait actions may be taken that turn after a Finisher
- REQ: Some Finishers specify a Failure effect (partial damage); critical failures do not trigger failure effects

### Paragraph — Confident Finisher

> 1 Action, Finisher trait. Make a Strike with a qualifying weapon (agile or finesse). On success, deals full precise strike finisher damage. On failure, deals half precise strike damage (no dice — half of flat bonus).

- REQ: Confident Finisher is the base 1-action Finisher learned at 1st level
- REQ: Success: Strike hits with full finisher precise strike damage
- REQ: Failure: Deals half precise strike damage (as flat value, not rolled)

### Paragraph — Opportune Riposte

> Reaction, gained at 3rd level. Trigger: A foe within reach critically fails a Strike against you. Effect: Make a melee Strike or attempt to Disarm the triggering foe.

- REQ: Opportune Riposte is a reaction gained at L3
- REQ: Triggers on a foe's critical failure on a Strike against the swashbuckler
- REQ: Effect choices: melee Strike against the foe, or Disarm the weapon that missed

### Paragraph — Vivacious Speed

> 3rd level. While you have panache, status bonus to Speed increases to +10 feet. Scales by +5 feet at L7/11/15/19 (max +30 feet at L19). Even without panache, you gain half this bonus rounded down to nearest 5-foot increment.

- REQ: Vivacious Speed replaces the basic panache speed bonus at L3
- REQ: Speed bonus while in panache: +10 ft (L3), +15 ft (L7), +20 ft (L11), +25 ft (L15), +30 ft (L19)
- REQ: Without panache: gain half the bonus rounded down to nearest 5-foot increment (passive partial benefit)

### Paragraph — Exemplary Finisher (L9)

> When a Finisher Strike hits, add a style-specific bonus effect:
> - Battledancer: Free Step after the finisher
> - Braggart: End the foe's temporary Demoralize immunity
> - Fencer: Foe is flat-footed until your next turn
> - Gymnast: Circumstance bonus to damage equal to 2x weapon damage dice (if foe is grabbed, restrained, or prone)
> - Wit: Foe takes –2 circumstance penalty to attack rolls against you until start of your next turn

- REQ: Exemplary Finisher added at L9; activates when a Finisher Strike hits
- REQ: Effect is style-specific (see list above); apply correct effect per style

## SECTION: Witch

### Paragraph — Witch Core Stats

> HP 6 + Con; Key Ability: Intelligence; Perception: Trained; Saves: Trained Fortitude, Trained Reflex, Expert Will; Weapons: Simple, Unarmed; Armor: None (Trained in Unarmored); Spellcasting: Tradition determined by patron theme

- REQ: Witch class has 6 HP + Con per level
- REQ: Witch key ability is Intelligence; spell DC and attack rolls use Int modifier
- REQ: Saves: Trained Fortitude, Trained Reflex, Expert Will
- REQ: Witch has no armor proficiency; trained only in unarmored defense
- REQ: Spellcasting tradition is determined by chosen patron theme (not fixed at class level)

### Paragraph — Patron System

> Witch selects a patron theme at 1st level. The patron determines: spellcasting tradition (arcane/divine/occult/primal), patron skill (trained), hex cantrip, and one granted spell automatically added to the familiar. Patron identity is intentionally vague/mysterious.

- REQ: Patron theme must be selected at character creation; cannot be changed later
- REQ: Patron theme determines: spell tradition, patron skill, hex cantrip, and familiar's granted spell
- REQ: Patron themes: Curse (occult), Fate (occult), Fervor (divine), Night (occult), Rune (arcane), Wild (primal), Winter (primal)
- REQ: Patron skill is added as a Trained skill for the witch

### Paragraph — Familiar Requirements

> Witch's familiar is a mandatory class feature (not optional). The familiar functions per Core Rulebook familiar rules but gains +1 bonus familiar ability at L1, +1 more at L6, L12, and L18. Familiar is the repository of all witch spells — witch prepares spells by communing with familiar each morning. If familiar dies, patron replaces it at next daily preparations; all spells are retained.

- REQ: Witch must have a familiar; it is a class-locked feature, not optional
- REQ: Witch familiar gains bonus familiar abilities at L1, L6, L12, L18 (one extra each)
- REQ: All witch spells are stored in the familiar; communing with familiar is required to prepare spells
- REQ: Familiar death does not erase known spells; a replacement familiar is provided at next daily prep
- REQ: The replacement familiar knows all spells the prior familiar knew

### Paragraph — Witch Spellcasting (Prepared)

> Witch uses prepared spellcasting (not spontaneous). At 1st level: 5 cantrips + 2 first-level slots. Familiar starts with 10 cantrips, 5 first-level spells, and 1 patron-granted spell. Each level gained, patron teaches familiar 2 new spells of any level the witch can cast. Familiar can also learn spells by consuming scrolls (1-hour process).

- REQ: Witch uses prepared (not spontaneous) spellcasting
- REQ: Familiar begins with 10 cantrips and 5 first-level spells (plus 1 patron-granted spell)
- REQ: Each class level gained: familiar learns 2 new spells chosen by the player from the tradition's spell list
- REQ: Familiar can learn spells by physically consuming scrolls (1 hour per spell)
- REQ: Witch can use Learn a Spell to create a consumable written version for familiar to absorb
- REQ: Two witch familiars can teach each other spells via Learn a Spell activity (both present, usual cost applies)
- REQ: Witch cannot prepare spells from another witch's familiar directly

### Paragraph — Hex Rules

> Hexes are focus spells. Only one hex may be cast per turn; attempts to cast a second hex on the same turn automatically fail. Focus pool starts at 1 Focus Point. Refocus = 10 minutes communing with familiar. Hex cantrips (granted by patron) do not cost Focus Points but still count toward the one-hex-per-turn limit.

- REQ: Hexes are focus spells; use the focus pool and focus spell rules
- REQ: Only one hex may be cast per turn; second hex cast fails with actions wasted
- REQ: Witch starts with focus pool of 1 Focus Point
- REQ: Refocus = 10 minutes communing with familiar; restores 1 Focus Point
- REQ: Hex cantrips do not cost Focus Points (free to cast) but still obey one-hex-per-turn restriction
- REQ: Hex cantrips are auto-heightened to half witch level rounded up (same as regular cantrips)
- REQ: Hex cantrips are in addition to the witch's prepared cantrips (separate pool)

### Paragraph — Witch Lesson System

> Witch lessons are learned via feats (Basic Lesson, Greater Lesson, Major Lesson). Each lesson grants: one new hex (focus spell) and teaches the familiar one new spell. Three tiers: Basic Lessons, Greater Lessons, Major Lessons.

- REQ: Implement witch lesson system as a tiered feat mechanism
- REQ: Each lesson grants one hex (added to focus spell options) and adds one spell to familiar
- REQ: Lesson tiers: Basic (available L2+), Greater (available L6+), Major (available L10+)
- REQ: Basic lessons: Dreams (veil of dreams hex + sleep), Elements (elemental betrayal + elemental spell choice), Life (life boost + spirit link), Protection (blood ward + mage armor), Vengeance (needle of vengeance + phantom pain)
- REQ: Greater lessons: Mischief (deceiver's cloak + mad monkeys), Shadow (malicious shadow + chilling darkness), Snow (personal blizzard + wall of wind)
- REQ: Major lessons: Death (curse of death + raise dead), Renewal (restorative moment + field of life)

## SECTION: Core Class Expansions

### Paragraph — Alchemist: Toxicologist Research Field

> New research field. Starts with 2 common 1st-level poison formulas. Can apply injury poisons as a single action (not 2-action). Can use class DC instead of item DC for infused poisons. Field Discovery (L5): 3 poisons per reagent batch. Greater Field Discovery (L15): Apply two poisons to same weapon (double poison rules).

- REQ: Toxicologist is a new research field option for Alchemist
- REQ: Starts with 2 common 1st-level alchemical poison formulas
- REQ: Applying injury poison costs 1 action (not 2 actions as standard)
- REQ: May substitute class DC for poison save DC when using infused poisons
- REQ: L5: Field Discovery allows creating 3 poisons per batch (instead of 2)
- REQ: L15: Greater Field Discovery allows applying two injury poisons to same weapon (cannot use perpetual poisons); combined as double poison with lower DC

### Paragraph — Barbarian: Superstition Instinct

> New instinct option. Anathema: must not willingly accept the effects of magic spells (including from allies). Cannot use items that cast spells. Gain powers that counter magic in exchange for this restriction.

- REQ: Superstition is a new instinct for Barbarian
- REQ: Anathema: willingly accepting magical spell effects (from any source) violates instinct
- REQ: Restriction includes spell-casting items (wands, scrolls that cast spells); does not include potions or non-spell magic item activations
- REQ: Continuing to associate with allies who repeatedly cast magic on the barbarian against their will is also anathema

### Paragraph — Bard: Warrior Muse

> New muse option. Grants Martial Performance feat and adds the fear spell to spell repertoire. New composition cantrip: Song of Strength (L2 feat). Enables wade-into-combat play style.

- REQ: Warrior is a new muse option for Bard
- REQ: Warrior muse grants Martial Performance feat and adds fear to repertoire
- REQ: Song of Strength is a composition cantrip unlocked via a L2 feat for warrior muse bards

### Paragraph — Champion: Evil Champions

> APG adds tenets and mechanics for evil-aligned champions. Champions of evil have their own code of tenets (hierarchy enforcement, merciless application of power). Uncommon access — GM must grant access. All other champion rules (reactions, devotion spells, cause structure) apply normally.

- REQ: Champion system supports evil-aligned options as Uncommon access
- REQ: Evil champion tenets require GM access grant
- REQ: Evil champions gain appropriate alignment-based champion's reaction and devotion spells (paralleling good champion structure)

### Paragraph — Rogue: New Rackets

> **Eldritch Trickster**: Choose a multiclass spellcasting archetype; gain its dedication feat for free (level requirement waived, other prerequisites apply). Magical Trickster prereq drops to L2. Can use spellcasting modifier as key ability.
> **Mastermind**: Uses Int-based Recall Knowledge to make foes flat-footed. Trained in Society + one knowledge skill. Can choose Intelligence as key ability. Flat-footed lasts until next turn on success, 1 minute on critical success.

- REQ: Eldritch Trickster is a new rogue racket that grants a free multiclass spellcasting archetype dedication
- REQ: Eldritch Trickster drops Magical Trickster feat to L2 (from L4)
- REQ: Mastermind is a new rogue racket using Intelligence; gains Society + one knowledge skill
- REQ: Mastermind: successful Recall Knowledge renders target flat-footed until next turn; critical success = 1 minute
- REQ: Both rackets allow Intelligence as the key ability score choice

### Paragraph — Sorcerer: New Bloodlines

> **Genie bloodline**: Arcane tradition; choose genie subtype (Janni/Djinni/Efreeti/Marid/Shaitan) which determines 2nd/5th/8th level granted spells. Initial/advanced/greater bloodline spells: genie's veil, heart's desire, wish-twisted form.
> **Nymph bloodline**: Primal tradition; initial/advanced/greater bloodline spells: nymph's token, blinding beauty, establish ward.

- REQ: Genie is a new sorcerer bloodline using arcane spell list
- REQ: Genie bloodline requires subtype selection (Janni, Djinni, Efreeti, Marid, Shaitan) at L1; determines certain granted spells
- REQ: Nymph is a new sorcerer bloodline using primal spell list
- REQ: Both bloodlines follow standard bloodline structure (granted spells, bloodline spells, blood magic effect)

### Paragraph — Wizard: Staff Nexus Arcane Thesis

> New arcane thesis. Begin with a makeshift staff containing 1 cantrip + 1 first-level spell from spellbook. Staff gains no charges from normal daily prep; wizard must expend spell slots to charge it. At L8, can expend 2 spells to add combined-level charges. At L16, can expend up to 3 spells.

- REQ: Staff Nexus is a new arcane thesis option for Wizard
- REQ: Wizard starts with a makeshift staff (1 cantrip + 1 first-level spell from spellbook)
- REQ: Makeshift staff gains charges only via expended spell slots (not standard staff charging rules)
- REQ: L8: May expend 2 spell slots; combined spell levels = charges added
- REQ: L16: May expend up to 3 spell slots; combined spell levels = charges added
- REQ: Can Craft makeshift staff into any other staff type (standard cost), retaining the two original spells
