# Pathfinder 2E: Character Creation Process

## Overview

Creating a character in Pathfinder 2nd Edition follows a structured 10-step process that builds your adventurer from concept to fully realized hero. This guide walks you through each step in detail, helping you create a character ready to face the dangers of the Age of Lost Omens.

## Understanding Ability Scores

Before diving into character creation, it's essential to understand the six ability scores that form the foundation of your character:

### The Six Ability Scores

**Physical Ability Scores:**
- **Strength (Str)**: Physical power and the ability to deal damage in melee combat
- **Dexterity (Dex)**: Agility, balance, and reflexes for avoiding danger and ranged attacks
- **Constitution (Con)**: Overall health, stamina, and vitality; determines your Hit Points

**Mental Ability Scores:**
- **Intelligence (Int)**: Raw knowledge, memory, and problem-solving ability
- **Wisdom (Wis)**: Awareness, intuition, and insight into people and situations
- **Charisma (Cha)**: Force of personality, charm, and persuasiveness

### Ability Modifiers

Each ability score has an associated modifier that you add to rolls. To calculate a modifier:
- Subtract 10 from the ability score
- Divide the result by 2 (round down)
- Formula: `(Ability Score - 10) ÷ 2`

**Ability Score to Modifier Table:**

| Score | Modifier | Score | Modifier | Score | Modifier |
|-------|----------|-------|----------|-------|----------|
| 1 | -5 | 10-11 | +0 | 20-21 | +5 |
| 2-3 | -4 | 12-13 | +1 | 22-23 | +6 |
| 4-5 | -3 | 14-15 | +2 | 24-25 | +7 |
| 6-7 | -2 | 16-17 | +3 | 26-27 | +8 |
| 8-9 | -1 | 18-19 | +4 | 28-29 | +9 |

### Ability Boosts and Flaws

During character creation, you'll apply **ability boosts** and **ability flaws** to your scores:
- An **ability boost** increases an ability score by 2
- If the score is already 18 or higher, it increases by only 1
- An **ability flaw** decreases an ability score by 2

**Important Rules:**
- All ability scores start at 10
- You cannot boost the same ability score more than once per step
- Ability boosts are key to building your character's strengths

## Character Creation Steps

### Step 1: Create a Concept

Begin by imagining the character you want to play. Consider their personality, background, and what drives them to adventure. This step is about creativity and storytelling—there are no rules to follow, just imagination!

**Questions to Consider:**
- **Personality**: Are they brave and bold, or cautious and calculating? Friendly or aloof? Serious or humorous?
- **Background**: What was their life like before adventuring? Were they raised in a city or countryside? Rich or poor?
- **Motivations**: Why did they become an adventurer? Seeking glory? Protecting the innocent? Searching for lost knowledge?
- **Relationships**: How do they interact with others? Are they a natural leader or a lone wolf?
- **Appearance**: What do they look like? How do they dress? Do they have any distinguishing features?
- **Values**: What do they believe in? What lines won't they cross?

**Examples:**
- A noble-born scholar who fled a corrupt family to seek the truth
- A street-smart rogue who steals from the rich to feed the poor
- A battle-hardened warrior seeking redemption for past failures
- A curious gnome inventor obsessed with discovering how magic works

**Tip**: Keep your concept flexible at this stage. As you make mechanical choices in the following steps, you might refine or adjust your character's concept to match their abilities.

### Step 2: Start Building Ability Scores

All six ability scores begin at 10, representing average human capability. Over the next several steps, you'll apply ability boosts (and possibly flaws) from your ancestry, background, class, and free choices. Record this starting point and prepare to track changes.

**Starting Point**: 
- Strength: 10
- Dexterity: 10
- Constitution: 10
- Intelligence: 10
- Wisdom: 10
- Charisma: 10

**What You'll Do in Later Steps:**
1. Apply ancestry boosts and flaws (Step 3)
2. Apply background boosts (Step 4)
3. Apply class key ability boost (Step 5)
4. Apply four free boosts (Step 6)

**Important Rule**: During any given step, you cannot apply more than one boost to the same ability score. This prevents min-maxing but allows significant specialization across all steps.

**Planning Ahead**: Think about what kind of character you want to play:
- **Martial warriors** typically want high Strength or Dexterity
- **Spellcasters** need high Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma (depending on class)
- **Everyone benefits** from Constitution (more Hit Points) and Dexterity (better AC and Reflex saves)
- **Skill specialists** want high scores in abilities related to their favorite skills


### Step 3: Select an Ancestry

Your ancestry represents the people your character belongs to—whether they're sturdy dwarves, graceful elves, cunning goblins, or something even more unusual. This is one of the most important character creation choices, as it affects your character throughout their entire adventuring career.

**What Your Ancestry Provides:**

**Hit Points**: Your ancestry grants a set amount of Hit Points at 1st level. This represents your natural hardiness and combines with your class's Hit Points.

**Size**: Most ancestries are Medium (roughly 5-8 feet tall), though some like gnomes, goblins, and halflings are Small (roughly 3-4 feet tall). Size affects how much you can lift and carry.

**Speed**: Your base movement speed in feet, usually 25 or 30 feet per action spent to Stride.

**Ability Boosts**: Your ancestry grants ability boosts, typically to two different abilities. Some are predetermined (like dwarves always boosting Constitution), while others are "free" boosts you can apply to any ability you choose.

**Ability Flaws**: Some ancestries have an ability flaw, representing

 a weakness common to that people. This reduces an ability score by 2.

**Languages**: You automatically know the Common language and your ancestry's language. High Intelligence gives you additional languages.

**Special Abilities**: Each ancestry has unique traits, such as darkvision, natural weapons, or resistances.

**Ancestry Feats**: At 1st level, you choose one ancestry feat, representing a special ability from your culture or heritage.

**Heritage**: Every ancestry has multiple heritages representing different subgroups or lineages. For example, elves might be arctic elves, cavern elves, or woodland elves.

**Available Ancestries:**

| Ancestry | HP | Size | Speed | Common Boosts | Flaw |
|----------|-----|------|-------|---------------|------|
| **Dwarf** | 10 | Medium | 20 ft | Con, Wis, Free | Cha |
| **Elf** | 6 | Medium | 30 ft | Dex, Int, Free | Con |
| **Gnome** | 8 | Small | 25 ft | Con, Cha, Free | Str |
| **Goblin** | 6 | Small | 25 ft | Dex, Cha, Free | Wis |
| **Halfling** | 6 | Small | 25 ft | Dex, Wis, Free | Str |
| **Human** | 8 | Medium | 25 ft | Two Free | None |
| **Mobians** | 8 | Medium | 25 ft | Dex, Free | None |
| **Fungians** | 10 | Medium | 25 ft | Con, Wis, Free | Dex |
| **Automaton** | 8 | Medium | 25 ft | Str, Free | None |
| **Undead** | 6 | Medium | 25 ft | Two Free | Con |
| **Talking Animal** | 6 | Small | 25 ft | Wis, Free | None |

**Choosing an Ancestry**: Consider both mechanics and story. Do you want to play a tough, tradition-bound dwarf? A curious, magical gnome? An unpredictable goblin? Think about how your ancestry fits your character concept.

**Heritage Selection**: After choosing your ancestry, select a heritage. These provide additional mechanical benefits and flavor. For example:
- **Dwarves**: Ancient-Blooded (resist magic), Forge (fire resistance), Rock (darkvision range increase), etc.
- **Elves**: Arctic (cold resistance), Cavern (darkvision), Seer (detect magic), Whisper (hearing range increase), Woodland (climb speed)
- **Humans**: Versatile heritage (gain a 1st-level general feat with no prerequisites)

**Ancestry Feat**: Choose one 1st-level ancestry feat. These represent special training or abilities from your culture.

### Step 4: Pick a Background

Your background describes your training and experience before you became an adventurer. Were you a scholarly acolyte? A hard-working farmhand? A cunning criminal? Your background shapes who your character was before the adventure began.

**What Backgrounds Provide:**

**Two Ability Boosts**: You choose which two abilities to boost. Select boosts that complement your planned class or shore up weak abilities. Remember, you can't boost the same ability more than once per step.

**Skill Training**: You become trained in a specific skill. Training represents formal instruction or practical experience, and grants a +2 proficiency bonus (plus your level) to that skill.

**Lore Skill**: You become trained in a specific Lore subcategory related to your background. Lore represents specialized knowledge, like Academia Lore, Engineering Lore, Mercantile Lore, etc.

**Skill Feat**: You gain a 1st-level skill feat, usually related to your background's skill. Skill feats grant special abilities when using skills.

**Choosing a Background**: Think about your character's life before adventuring. What skills would they have learned? The background should complement both your character concept and your intended class.

**Example**: A character who plans to be a cleric might choose the Acolyte background (religious training), while a future rogue might pick Criminal or Street Urchin for stealth expertise.

### Step 5: Choose a Class

Your class is the most important decision you'll make, representing your character's adventuring profession and primary area of expertise. It determines the majority of your abilities, proficiencies, and what you do during adventures.

**What Your Class Provides:**

**Key Ability Score**: Each class has one or two key ability scores that are most important for that class. Your class grants an ability boost to one key ability of your choice.

**Hit Points**: Each class grants a set number of HP every time you gain a level (including 1st level). This combines with your ancestry HP and Constitution modifier.

**Initial Proficiencies**: Your class determines your starting proficiency ranks in:
- Perception
- Saving throws (Fortitude, Reflex, Will)
- Attacks (various weapon groups)
- Defenses (armor types)
- Class DC (for class abilities)
- Spells (if a spellcaster)
- Skills (a specific number of skills)

**Class Features**: Special abilities unique to your class. These might include spells, rage, sneak attack, or other signature abilities.

**Class Feats**: At 1st level and every even level thereafter, you gain a class feat, choosing from options available to your class.

**Available Classes:**

| Class | Key Ability | HP/Level | Role | Complexity |
|-------|-------------|----------|------|------------|
| **Alchemist** | Intelligence | 8 | Support/Striker | Moderate |
| **Barbarian** | Strength | 12 | Tank/Striker | Low |
| **Bard** | Charisma | 8 | Support/Caster | Moderate |
| **Champion** | Strength or Dex | 10 | Tank/Support | Low |
| **Cleric** | Wisdom | 8 | Support/Caster | Low |
| **Druid** | Wisdom | 8 | Caster/Support | Moderate |
| **Fighter** | Str or Dex | 10 | Striker | Low |
| **Monk** | Str or Dex | 10 | Striker | Moderate |
| **Ranger** | Str or Dex | 10 | Striker | Moderate |
| **Rogue** | Dex, Str, or Cha | 8 | Striker/Skill | Moderate |
| **Sorcerer** | Charisma | 6 | Caster | Moderate |
| **Wizard** | Intelligence | 6 | Caster | High |
| **Inventor** | Intelligence | 8 | Support/Striker | High |
| **Gunslinger** | Dexterity | 8 | Striker | Moderate |
| **Summoner** | Charisma | 10 | Caster/Pet | High |

**Class Roles:**
- **Tank**: High HP and AC, protects allies
- **Striker**: Deals high damage to enemies
- **Caster**: Uses magic to control battlefield and solve problems
- **Support**: Buffs allies, debuffs enemies, provides healing
- **Skill**: Excels at non-combat challenges

**Brief Class Descriptions:**

**Alchemist**: Create potent alchemical items including elixirs, bombs, and mutagens. Masters of improvisation and preparation.

**Barbarian**: Tap into primal rage to become an unstoppable warrior. Highest Hit Points and devastating damage.

**Bard**: Use occult magic and performances to inspire allies and confound foes. Masters of versatility and support.

**Champion**: Holy warriors who defend their deity's ideals. Tough, protective, and blessed with divine power.

**Cleric**: Channel your deity's power to heal, harm, and support. Divine spellcasters with excellent support abilities.

**Druid**: Primal spellcasters who can shapeshift into animals. Masters of nature and the wild.

**Fighter**: Unmatched weapon masters with the highest attack accuracy. Simple but devastatingly effective.

**Monk**: Martial artists with supernatural ki powers. Mobile strikers who focus on unarmed combat.

**Ranger**: Wilderness warriors who hunt prey and survive in the wild. Excellent at tracking and ranged combat.

**Rogue**: Cunning strikers who use stealth and tricks to deal massive sneak attack damage. Also skill masters.

**Sorcerer**: Born with innate magic from a bloodline. Flexible spellcasters with powerful focus spells.

**Wizard**: Scholarly arcane spellcasters who learn magic through study. The most versatile casters.

**Inventor**: Create and customize gadgets and constructs. Blend technology and magic.

**Gunslinger**: Master of firearms and crossbows with incredible precision and unique combat styles.

**Summoner**: Bond with a powerful eidolon companion. You and your summoned creature fight as one.

**Choosing Your Class**: Consider your play style and group role. Do you want to be in the thick of combat or supporting from behind? Solving problems with steel or with spells? Make sure your choice fits both your character concept and your group's needs.

### Step 6: Determine Ability Scores
Apply all boosts and flaws, then apply 4 free ability boosts:
1. Apply ability boosts from ancestry
2. Apply ability boosts from background
3. Apply ability boost from class
4. Apply 4 additional free ability boosts (each to a different score)

### Step 7: Record Class Details
Fill in your character sheet with:
- **Hit Points**: Add ancestry HP + class HP + Constitution modifier
- **Proficiencies**: Mark all proficiency ranks granted by class
- **Class Features**: Note special abilities
- **Initial Class Feat**: Record your chosen feat
- **Spells** (if applicable): Note cantrips and 1st-level spells

### Step 8: Buy Equipment
- Start with **15 gold pieces**
- Purchase weapons, armor, and gear
- Track Bulk (encumbrance system)
- Note that some classes provide specific starting equipment

### Step 9: Calculate Modifiers
Calculate all derived statistics:
- **Perception**: Use Perception proficiency + Wisdom modifier
- **Saving Throws**: Calculate Fortitude, Reflex, and Will saves
- **Melee Strikes**: Note attack bonus and damage for melee weapons
- **Ranged Strikes**: Note attack bonus and damage for ranged weapons
- **Skill Modifiers**: Calculate modifier for each skill
- **Armor Class (AC)**: AC = 10 + Dexterity modifier + proficiency + item bonus

### Step 10: Finishing Details
Add final character details:
- **Alignment**: Choose your moral and ethical outlook
- **Deity**: If worshipping a god
- **Age**: Determine character's age
- **Gender & Pronouns**: Define identity
- **Appearance**: Describe looks and style
- **Personality**: Define traits and quirks

## Proficiency Ranks

Mark proficiency as one of these ranks:

| Rank | Bonus | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Untrained** | +0 | No training (may be unable to attempt some tasks) |
| **Trained** | +2 + Level | Received training |
| **Expert** | +4 + Level | Extensive training and experience |
| **Master** | +6 + Level | World-class expertise |
| **Legendary** | +8 + Level | True mastery, among the best in the world |

## Ability Score Guidelines

### Starting Scores (After All Boosts)
- **18**: Your class's key ability (if you maximize it)
- **16**: Secondary important abilities
- **14**: Tertiary abilities
- **12**: Average abilities
- **10**: Unimportant abilities
- **8 or lower**: If you took a flaw without offsetting it

### Ability Modifiers
To calculate modifier: `(Ability Score - 10) ÷ 2` (round down)

| Score | Modifier |
|-------|----------|
| 8 | -1 |
| 10-11 | +0 |
| 12-13 | +1 |
| 14-15 | +2 |
| 16-17 | +3 |
| 18-19 | +4 |

## Alternative: Rolling for Ability Scores

If your GM allows, you can roll randomly:

**Method**: Roll 4d6, drop the lowest die, sum the remaining 3
- Repeat 6 times
- Assign results to your 6 abilities in any order
- Then proceed with normal ancestry, background, and class boosts

## Character Creation Checklist

- [ ] Create concept
- [ ] Start with all abilities at 10
- [ ] Select ancestry (apply boosts/flaws, choose heritage and feat)
- [ ] Pick background (apply boosts, note skills)
- [ ] Choose class (note key ability, HP, features)
- [ ] Determine final ability scores (apply all boosts)
- [ ] Record class details (HP, proficiencies, features)
- [ ] Buy equipment (15 gp budget)
- [ ] Calculate all modifiers (AC, saves, skills, etc.)
- [ ] Add finishing details (alignment, deity, description)
