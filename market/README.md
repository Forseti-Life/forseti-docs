## Market Analysis Framework

### 1. Market Sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**  
The entire revenue opportunity if you achieved 100% market share.

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**  
The segment of TAM you can realistically target with your product.

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**  
The portion of SAM you can realistically capture in the near term.

**Document**: `market-sizing.md`

---

### 2. Competitive Analysis

**Direct Competitors**  
Products solving the exact same problem for the same customers.

**Indirect Competitors**  
Alternative solutions to the same problem.

**Substitute Products**  
Different approaches customers use instead of any dedicated solution.

**Document**: `competitive-analysis.md`

---

**Core Elements**:

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Beachhead Market

**Definition**: The first, small market you'll dominate before expanding.

**Characteristics of Good Beachhead**:
- Specific, well-defined customer segment
- Problem is acute (customers desperate for solution)
- You can reach them affordably
- Small enough to dominate, big enough to matter

**Forseti Beachhead**: _[To be defined]_

### Crossing the Chasm

**Technology Adoption Lifecycle**:
1. Innovators (2.5%)
2. Early Adopters (13.5%)
3. Early Majority (34%) ← **The Chasm**
4. Late Majority (34%)
5. Laggards (16%)

**The Chasm**: The gap between early adopters (who love new tech) and the early majority (who need proven solutions).

**Strategy**: Focus on one beachhead market, dominate it completely, then expand.
