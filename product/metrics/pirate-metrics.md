**Last Updated**: 2024-12-13

## Overview

The AARRR framework (Pirate Metrics) was developed by Dave McClure to help startups focus on the metrics that matter most. It tracks the customer lifecycle from acquisition to referral.

**Why "Pirate" Metrics?** AARRR sounds like a pirate saying "Arrr!" 🏴‍☠️

---

## The AARRR Framework

```
Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral
    ↓            ↓            ↓           ↓          ↓
 Users find   First great  Users come  Users pay  Users refer
    you       experience     back                  others
```

### Lifetime Value (LTV) Calculation

```
LTV = ARPU × Customer Lifetime (months)

ARPU = Monthly Revenue / Active Users
Customer Lifetime = 1 / Monthly Churn Rate

Example:
ARPU = $1,000 / 200 users = $5
Churn = 5% per month
Customer Lifetime = 1 / 0.05 = 20 months
LTV = $5 × 20 = $100
```

**Target LTV**: $[Y]

### Payback Period

**How long to recover CAC?**

```
Payback Period = CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin %)

Example:
CAC = $30
ARPU = $5
Gross Margin = 80%
Payback Period = $30 / ($5 × 0.80) = 7.5 months
```

### Viral Coefficient (K) Calculation

```
K = (# of invites sent per user) × (conversion rate of invites)

Example:
Average user sends 5 invites
20% of invites convert to signups
K = 5 × 0.20 = 1.0

K > 1 = Viral growth (each user brings >1 new user)
K < 1 = Paid growth required
```

## 📊 North Star Metric

### What is a North Star Metric?

The **single metric** that best captures the core value you deliver to customers. It:
- Reflects customer value (not vanity metric)
- Drives long-term growth
- Aligns entire company

## Actionable vs. Vanity Metrics

### Vanity Metrics ❌
Metrics that look good but don't drive action:
- Total registered users (includes inactive)
- Total page views (without engagement context)
- App downloads (without activation)
- Social media followers (without engagement)

### Actionable Metrics ✅
Metrics that inform decisions:
- Weekly active users (shows engagement)
- Retention rate (shows product-market fit)
- Conversion rate (shows funnel performance)
- NPS (shows customer satisfaction)

**Rule**: If the metric doesn't change your behavior, it's vanity.
