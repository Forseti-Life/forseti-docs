## What is a Pivot?

A **pivot** is a structured course correction designed to test a new fundamental hypothesis about the product, strategy, or business model.

> "A pivot is a special kind of change designed to test a new fundamental hypothesis about the product, business model, and engine of growth." - Eric Ries, The Lean Startup

**Key Point**: A pivot is NOT giving up. It's **changing strategy while staying grounded in learning**.

---

## When to Pivot

### Signals You Might Need to Pivot

**Product/Market Fit Signals**:
- [ ] Retention curve keeps declining (no flattening)
- [ ] Customers try product once and never return
- [ ] NPS consistently below 30
- [ ] Users say "nice to have" but not "must have"
- [ ] Low activation rate (<20%) despite optimization

**Customer Feedback Signals**:
- [ ] Customers keep asking for completely different features
- [ ] Problem you're solving isn't painful enough
- [ ] Solution doesn't solve the problem well enough
- [ ] Wrong customer segment (early adopters don't match assumptions)

**Business Model Signals**:
- [ ] Can't achieve sustainable unit economics (LTV < CAC)
- [ ] Market too small to support business
- [ ] Can't find scalable acquisition channel
- [ ] Competitors have insurmountable advantage

**Team Signals**:
- [ ] No progress after multiple iterations
- [ ] Experiments consistently invalidate hypotheses
- [ ] Team losing confidence/motivation

## Pivot or Persevere Decision Framework

### Innovation Accounting Milestones

**Milestone 1: Establish Baseline**
- Set up metrics, get first cohort data
- **Decision**: Do we have enough data to learn?

**Milestone 2: Tune the Engine**
- Optimize features, improve metrics incrementally
- **Decision**: Are metrics improving towards targets?

**Milestone 3: Pivot or Persevere**
- After multiple iterations, review overall progress
- **Decision**: Continue current strategy or change?

## Types of Pivots

Eric Ries identifies 10 types of pivots:

### 1. Zoom-In Pivot

**Definition**: What was previously a single feature becomes the whole product.

**Example**:
- **Before**: Full safety app with map, alerts, social, route planning
- **After**: Focus entirely on background monitoring and alerts

**When**: Users only care about one specific feature

---

### 2. Zoom-Out Pivot

**Definition**: What was the whole product becomes a single feature of a larger product.

**Example**:
- **Before**: Safety map app
- **After**: Safety map becomes one feature in broader urban mobility platform

**When**: Product alone isn't compelling, but adds value as part of something bigger

---

### 3. Customer Segment Pivot

**Definition**: Same solution, different customers.

**Example**:
- **Before**: Targeting urban commuters
- **After**: Targeting real estate agents (B2B)

**When**: Solution works, but wrong customer segment

**Rationale**: Real estate agents have stronger willingness to pay, easier to reach

---

### 4. Customer Need Pivot

**Definition**: Same customer, different problem.

**Example**:
- **Before**: Solving "personal safety when walking"
- **After**: Solving "property investment risk assessment"

**When**: You learn customers have a more important problem

---

### 5. Platform Pivot

**Definition**: Change from application to platform or vice versa.

**Example**:
- **Before**: AmISafe app (application)
- **After**: Safety data API for other apps to integrate (platform)

**When**: More value in enabling others than being the end solution

---

### 6. Business Architecture Pivot

**Definition**: Change from high margin/low volume to low margin/high volume, or vice versa.

**Example**:
- **Before**: B2C freemium ($4.99/month, need millions of users)
- **After**: B2B enterprise ($5,000/month, need 100 customers)

**When**: Unit economics don't work in current model

---

### 7. Value Capture Pivot

**Definition**: Change how you monetize (pricing model).

**Example**:
- **Before**: Subscription model ($4.99/user/month)
- **After**: Data licensing model (sell aggregated data to cities, insurance)

**When**: Customers won't pay, but data/insights are valuable to others

---

### 8. Engine of Growth Pivot

**Definition**: Change growth strategy (viral, sticky, paid).

**Example**:
- **Before**: Paid acquisition (Google/Facebook ads)
- **After**: Viral growth (referral program, network effects)

**When**: Current growth engine too expensive or not scalable

---

### 9. Channel Pivot

**Definition**: Change distribution/sales channel.

**Example**:
- **Before**: Direct to consumer (app stores)
- **After**: Through real estate companies (white label)

**When**: Current channel has poor conversion or is too expensive

---

### 10. Technology Pivot

**Definition**: Use different technology to achieve the same solution.

**Example**:
- **Before**: Client-side processing, heavy mobile app
- **After**: Server-side processing, lightweight web app

**When**: Technology constraints limit growth or UX

## Pivot Checklist

### Before Pivoting

- [ ] Ran enough experiments to validate/invalidate hypothesis (minimum 5-10)
- [ ] Talked to enough customers (minimum 30-50 interviews)
- [ ] Gave current strategy enough time (minimum 3 months or 3 Build-Measure-Learn cycles)
- [ ] Identified specific hypothesis that was invalidated
- [ ] Have new hypothesis to test
- [ ] Team alignment on decision
- [ ] Enough runway for new direction (6+ months)

### During Pivot

- [ ] Communicate clearly to team
- [ ] Update strategy documents (Lean Canvas, roadmap)
- [ ] Notify existing users (if applicable)
- [ ] Pause or sunset features that don't fit new direction
- [ ] Define new success metrics
- [ ] Plan first experiments in new direction

### After Pivot

- [ ] Track new metrics
- [ ] Validate new hypotheses
- [ ] Compare results to old direction
- [ ] Document learnings
- [ ] Adjust as needed

## Pivot Examples (Famous Companies)

### YouTube
- **Before**: Video dating site
- **Pivot**: General video sharing platform
- **Type**: Customer Need Pivot

### Instagram
- **Before**: Location check-in app (Burbn)
- **Pivot**: Photo sharing app
- **Type**: Zoom-In Pivot (photo feature became entire product)

### Twitter
- **Before**: Podcasting platform (Odeo)
- **Pivot**: Microblogging service
- **Type**: Customer Need Pivot

### Slack
- **Before**: Gaming company (Glitch)
- **Pivot**: Team communication tool (internal tool became product)
- **Type**: Zoom-In Pivot

### Groupon
- **Before**: Collective action platform
- **Pivot**: Daily deals
- **Type**: Customer Need Pivot

## When NOT to Pivot

### False Signals

**Don't pivot if**:
- You've only run 1-2 experiments
- You haven't talked to enough customers
- You're just tired or discouraged
- You haven't given enough time (< 3 months)
- Metrics are improving, just slower than expected
- You're comparing to unrealistic benchmarks

**Instead**: Persevere with adjustments
