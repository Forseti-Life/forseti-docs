**Version**: 1.0  
**Last Updated**: December 13, 2024  
**Status**: 🟢 Live Beta Testing

## Persona Overview

**Name**: Sarah Martinez  
**Age**: 28  
**Location**: St. Louis, MO (Midtown/Downtown area)  
**Occupation**: Marketing Coordinator  
**Lifestyle**: Urban professional, relies on walking + public transit, lives alone  

**Pain Points**:
- Feels unsafe walking alone at night (especially after work events)
- Doesn't know which routes/neighborhoods to avoid
- Reads crime news but it's not actionable or location-specific
- Friends share anecdotes but information is fragmented
- Wants to explore city but anxiety limits her activities

**Goals**:
- Feel confident and safe in daily activities
- Make informed decisions about where to go and how to get there
- Reduce anxiety without becoming paranoid
- Explore new restaurants, venues, events without fear

## Journey Map: Sarah's First Experience with Forseti

### Discovery Phase

#### 1. Problem Trigger (Day 0)
**Context**: Sarah walks home from dinner with friends around 10pm. Hears shouting and sees police lights two blocks away. Feels uneasy and takes a longer, well-lit route home.

**Emotional State**: 😰 Anxious, frustrated, wishes she had better information

**Actions**:
- Googles "St. Louis crime map" and "safe walking routes St. Louis"
- Finds generic crime maps but they're outdated or require zooming/clicking multiple times
- Discovers Forseti.life in search results
- **First Impression**: Homepage headline resonates - "Know before you go - Hyperlocal safety intelligence"

---

### Activation Phase

#### 2. Landing Page (Activation Start)
**Context**: Sarah arrives at forseti.life website from Google search

**What She Sees**:
- Clear value proposition: "Hyperlocal safety intelligence for St. Louis"
- Interactive safety map immediately visible (no signup wall)
- St. Louis metro area with color-coded hexagons
- Her neighborhood visible on the map

**Emotional State**: 🤔 Curious, cautiously optimistic

**Actions**:
- Reads brief "How It Works" section
- Notices H3 hexagons and z-score methodology
- Appreciates transparency about data sources

---

#### 3. Aha Moment (Activation)
**Context**: Sarah explores the interactive map

**What She Does**:
1. Zooms to her apartment building in Midtown
2. Clicks on hexagon to see z-score: 1.2 (moderate risk)
3. Traces her typical walking route to Metro station
4. Clicks hexagons along two alternate routes:
   - **Route A** (usual path): z-scores 0.8 → 0.9 → 1.1 (low to moderate)
   - **Route B** (shortcut through alley): z-scores 1.8 → 2.3 → 2.1 (elevated risk)

**Aha Moment**: 💡 "This is exactly what I need! I can see which streets are safer!"

**Emotional State**: 😊 Excited, validated, feels empowered

**Key Insight**: Sarah realizes she's been taking the riskier shortcut unknowingly. The visual hexagon map makes it immediately clear which route is safer.

---

#### 4. Signup Decision (Activation Continue)
**Context**: Sarah wants proactive alerts while walking

**What Triggers Signup**:
- Sees CTA: "Get Mobile Alerts - Know when you enter higher-risk areas"
- Reads: "7-day free trial, cancel anytime, $4.99/month after"
- Thinks: "If this works, it's worth way more than $5/month for peace of mind"

**Actions**:
1. Clicks "Sign Up for Alerts"
2. Quick registration form (email, password, <2 minutes)
3. Email verification
4. Prompted to download "AmISafe by Forseti" mobile app

**Emotional State**: 😊 Optimistic, willing to try

---

#### 5. Mobile Onboarding (Activation Complete)
**Context**: Sarah downloads AmISafe app from iOS App Store

**Onboarding Flow**:
1. **Login**: Uses website credentials, seamless authentication
2. **Location Permission**: 
   - App explains: "We need location access to monitor your safety"
   - Sarah grants "Allow While Using"
   - App prompts for "Allow Always" for background monitoring
   - Sarah approves (trusts the value proposition)
3. **Premium Trial Setup**:
   - Automatically enrolled in 7-day free trial
   - No credit card required upfront
4. **Alert Configuration**:
   - **Threshold Selection**: Sarah chooses z-score 2.0 (moderate risk)
     - Lower = more sensitive alerts
     - Higher = only high-risk areas
   - **Cooldown Period**: Sets 15-minute cooldown (won't spam with alerts)
5. **Confirmation Screen**:
   - "You're protected! We'll alert you if you enter higher-risk areas."
   - Tutorial: "Tap any notification to see your location on the safety map"

**Emotional State**: 😌 Relieved, feels safer already

## Failure Modes & Risk Mitigation

### Potential Drop-Off Points

#### 1. Landing Page → Aha Moment Failure
**Risk**: Sarah doesn't understand hexagons or z-scores, gets confused, leaves

**Mitigation**:
- Clear "How It Works" tooltip on first visit
- Simple color coding (green = safe, red = risky)
- Avoid jargon in initial experience

---

#### 2. Aha Moment → Signup Failure
**Risk**: Sarah likes the map but doesn't see value in mobile alerts, doesn't sign up

**Mitigation**:
- Emphasize proactive alerts vs. reactive checking
- Show example notification in onboarding
- Offer 7-day free trial (low friction)

---

#### 3. Signup → Location Permission Failure
**Risk**: Sarah denies location permission, can't use background monitoring, doesn't see value

**Mitigation**:
- Explain exactly why location is needed ("to alert you in real-time")
- Show example use case before asking for permission
- Fallback: Still allow manual map checking if permissions denied

---

#### 4. First Alert → Trust Failure
**Risk**: First alert is false positive or inaccurate, Sarah loses trust, uninstalls

**Mitigation**:
- Conservative z-score thresholds (default 2.0, not 1.0)
- Allow user to customize sensitivity
- Provide context: "Based on last 90 days of data" in notification

---

#### 5. Trial End → Conversion Failure
**Risk**: Sarah didn't receive enough value during trial, doesn't convert

**Mitigation**:
- Ensure at least 1-2 alerts during trial (validate usefulness)
- Send email mid-trial: "You're safer with Forseti - here's what we've protected you from"
- Remind her of value before trial ends
- Offer annual plan discount ($49.99/year = $4.16/month)

## Learnings & Iteration Opportunities

### What This Journey Teaches Us

1. **Aha Moment is Critical**: Sarah's route comparison (z-score 0.8 vs. 2.3) was the moment she understood the value. We need to make this moment obvious for all users.

2. **Background Monitoring is the Moat**: Without proactive alerts, Forseti is just another crime map. The background service is what creates stickiness and justifies the subscription price.

3. **Trust is Earned Incrementally**: Sarah didn't trust the system immediately. First alert that worked → trust increased. Consistency over weeks → full trust.

4. **Price Anchoring Works**: $4.99/month feels cheap when compared to:
   - One Uber ride ($10-15)
   - Peace of mind (priceless)
   - Alternatives (none with this feature set)

5. **Advocacy is Natural**: When product genuinely helps, users evangelize organically. Sarah referred friends without being asked because she felt safer and wanted others to benefit.

---

### Iteration Ideas Based on Sarah's Journey

#### Short-term Optimizations (Q1 2025)
- **Onboarding Tutorial**: Add interactive walkthrough showing route comparison (simulate Sarah's Aha Moment)
- **Alert Context**: Include "You've received X alerts in the past 30 days" in trial reminder email
- **Social Proof**: Add testimonials from users like Sarah on landing page

#### Medium-term Features (Q2-Q3 2025)
- **Saved Locations**: Let Sarah save "Home" and "Work" to quick-check safety for daily routes
- **Route Comparison Tool**: Side-by-side comparison of 2-3 routes with average z-scores
- **Friend Referrals**: Incentive program (refer 3 friends → 1 month free)

#### Long-term Vision (Q4 2025+)
- **Multi-City Expansion**: Sarah wants to use it in Chicago, NYC when traveling
- **Crime Type Filtering**: Sarah's feature request - filter by violent, property, drug crimes
- **Route Planning Integration**: Google Maps plugin showing safety along route
