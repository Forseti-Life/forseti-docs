# Institution Types & Classifications

**Last Updated**: January 10, 2026  
**Purpose**: Define the taxonomy of institution types supported by Forseti platform  
**Audience**: Product team, sales, institution owners

---

## Overview

The Forseti Institutional Management platform supports a wide range of institution types, each with unique operational requirements, regulatory compliance needs, and participant management approaches. This document defines each institution type, their characteristics, typical use cases, and platform considerations.

---

## Institution Type Taxonomy

### Level 1: Primary Categories (8)
1. Educational Institutions
2. Healthcare Facilities
3. Child Care Centers
4. Residential Care Facilities
5. Correctional & Reentry Facilities
6. Adult Day Programs
7. Treatment & Recovery Centers
8. Community & Recreation Centers

### Level 2: Subcategories
Each primary category contains multiple specialized subtypes with specific requirements.

---

## 1. Educational Institutions

**Primary Characteristics**:
- Curriculum-based instruction
- Age-grouped participants
- Academic progress tracking
- Accreditation requirements
- State education department oversight

### Subcategories

#### 1.1 Private K-12 Schools
- **Participants**: Ages 5-18
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:15 to 1:25 depending on grade level
- **Regulations**: State education department, accreditation bodies
- **Key Features**: Gradebook, attendance, parent portal, transcript management
- **Licensing**: State educational facility license
- **Average Size**: 50-500 students

#### 1.2 Preschools & Pre-K Programs
- **Participants**: Ages 3-5
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:8 to 1:12
- **Regulations**: State childcare/education hybrid regulations
- **Key Features**: Developmental milestones, daily reports, nap tracking
- **Licensing**: Preschool license (varies by state)
- **Average Size**: 25-150 children

#### 1.3 Tutoring & Learning Centers
- **Participants**: Ages 5-18 (sometimes adults)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:1 to 1:6
- **Regulations**: Generally lighter regulatory burden
- **Key Features**: Scheduling, progress tracking, billing
- **Licensing**: Business license (education license often not required)
- **Average Size**: 20-200 students

#### 1.4 Vocational & Trade Schools
- **Participants**: Ages 16+ (often adults)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:10 to 1:20
- **Regulations**: State board of education, accreditation, industry certifications
- **Key Features**: Skills tracking, certification management, job placement
- **Licensing**: Vocational education license
- **Average Size**: 50-500 students

#### 1.5 Adult Education Centers
- **Participants**: Adults 18+
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:15 to 1:30
- **Regulations**: State adult education requirements
- **Key Features**: Course enrollment, competency tracking
- **Licensing**: Adult education license or business license
- **Average Size**: 100-1000 students

#### 1.6 Specialty Schools
- **Types**: Art schools, music schools, dance academies, sports training facilities
- **Participants**: Ages vary (typically 5-18)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:5 to 1:15
- **Regulations**: Generally lighter, may need specialized certifications
- **Key Features**: Skill progression tracking, performance management, competitions
- **Licensing**: Business license, specialty certifications
- **Average Size**: 30-300 students

## 2. Healthcare Facilities

**Primary Characteristics**:
- Medical service delivery
- HIPAA compliance required
- Licensed medical professionals
- Health department oversight
- Patient (participant) care plans

### Subcategories

#### 2.1 Outpatient Clinics
- **Participants**: All ages, appointment-based
- **Staff Ratios**: Varies by specialty
- **Regulations**: State health department, HIPAA, medical board
- **Key Features**: Appointment scheduling, medical records, billing/insurance
- **Licensing**: Medical facility license
- **Average Size**: 500-5000 patients

#### 2.2 Physical Therapy Centers
- **Participants**: All ages, typically adults
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:2 to 1:4 patients per therapist
- **Regulations**: State PT board, HIPAA
- **Key Features**: Treatment plans, progress notes, exercise tracking
- **Licensing**: PT facility license
- **Average Size**: 100-1000 patients

#### 2.3 Mental Health & Counseling Centers
- **Participants**: All ages (separate programs for children/adults)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:1 (individual therapy), 1:8 (group therapy)
- **Regulations**: State mental health licensing, HIPAA, crisis protocols
- **Key Features**: Session notes, treatment plans, crisis management
- **Licensing**: Mental health facility license
- **Average Size**: 100-500 clients

#### 2.4 Rehabilitation Centers (Medical)
- **Participants**: Adults recovering from injury/surgery
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:4 to 1:6
- **Regulations**: State health department, HIPAA, medical board
- **Key Features**: Treatment protocols, progress tracking, discharge planning
- **Licensing**: Rehabilitation facility license
- **Average Size**: 50-200 patients

#### 2.5 Dialysis Centers
- **Participants**: Adults with kidney disease
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:3 to 1:4 patients per nurse
- **Regulations**: CMS, state health department, HIPAA
- **Key Features**: Treatment scheduling, vital signs monitoring, medical supplies tracking
- **Licensing**: Dialysis facility license
- **Average Size**: 50-150 patients

#### 2.6 Dental & Orthodontic Practices
- **Participants**: All ages
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:1 to 1:2 per dentist
- **Regulations**: State dental board, HIPAA
- **Key Features**: Treatment plans, imaging management, appointment scheduling
- **Licensing**: Dental practice license
- **Average Size**: 500-3000 patients

---

## 3. Child Care Centers

**Primary Characteristics**:
- Children under age 6 primarily
- High staff-to-child ratios required
- Strict safety and health regulations
- Daily care vs. educational focus
- State childcare licensing

### Subcategories

#### 3.1 Infant & Toddler Centers
- **Participants**: Ages 6 weeks to 3 years
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:3 to 1:5 (strictly enforced)
- **Regulations**: State childcare licensing, health department
- **Key Features**: Diaper tracking, feeding logs, nap schedules, daily photos
- **Licensing**: Infant care license (specialized)
- **Average Size**: 20-60 infants/toddlers

#### 3.2 Full-Day Child Care Centers
- **Participants**: Ages 6 weeks to 5 years
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:4 to 1:12 depending on age
- **Regulations**: State childcare licensing, CACFP (food program)
- **Key Features**: Daily reports, meal tracking, activities, parent communication
- **Licensing**: Child care center license
- **Average Size**: 40-150 children

#### 3.3 After-School Programs
- **Participants**: Ages 5-12 (school-age)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:10 to 1:15
- **Regulations**: State childcare or education department
- **Key Features**: Pick-up coordination, homework help tracking, activity scheduling
- **Licensing**: School-age care license
- **Average Size**: 30-100 children

#### 3.4 Drop-In Child Care
- **Participants**: Ages 6 months to 12 years
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:6 to 1:10
- **Regulations**: State childcare licensing
- **Key Features**: Flexible scheduling, quick check-in/out, activity tracking
- **Licensing**: Child care license
- **Average Size**: 20-50 children

---

## 4. Residential Care Facilities

**Primary Characteristics**:
- 24/7 supervision and care
- Overnight stays (temporary or permanent)
- Room and board provided
- Complex licensing requirements
- Higher liability and insurance needs

### Subcategories

#### 4.1 Group Homes (Children & Youth)
- **Participants**: Ages 6-21 (foster care, behavioral health)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:4 to 1:8 (24/7 coverage)
- **Regulations**: State social services, child welfare
- **Key Features**: Behavior tracking, case management, education coordination, family visits
- **Licensing**: Residential childcare facility license
- **Average Size**: 6-16 residents

#### 4.2 Assisted Living Facilities
- **Participants**: Seniors 55+ needing daily living support
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:8 to 1:15 depending on acuity
- **Regulations**: State aging/health department
- **Key Features**: Medication management, activity calendar, dining services, care plans
- **Licensing**: Assisted living license
- **Average Size**: 20-100 residents

#### 4.3 Sober Living Homes
- **Participants**: Adults 18+ in recovery
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:8 to 1:12 (may be peer-run)
- **Regulations**: State substance abuse authority, housing authority
- **Key Features**: Drug testing, attendance at meetings, chore schedules, accountability
- **Licensing**: Sober living certification (varies by state)
- **Average Size**: 6-20 residents

#### 4.4 Transitional Housing (Youth Aging Out)
- **Participants**: Young adults 18-24 transitioning from foster care
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:6 to 1:10
- **Regulations**: State social services, housing authority
- **Key Features**: Life skills tracking, employment support, education coordination
- **Licensing**: Transitional housing license
- **Average Size**: 8-30 residents

#### 4.5 Memory Care Facilities
- **Participants**: Seniors with Alzheimer's/dementia
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:4 to 1:6 (secured facility)
- **Regulations**: State health department, specialized dementia care requirements
- **Key Features**: Medication management, behavior tracking, secured entry/exit, activity engagement
- **Licensing**: Memory care facility license
- **Average Size**: 20-60 residents

---

## 5. Correctional & Reentry Facilities

**Primary Characteristics**:
- Restricted freedom of movement
- Security and safety protocols
- Justice system coordination
- Behavior management focus
- Heavy regulatory oversight

### Subcategories

#### 5.1 Juvenile Detention Centers
- **Participants**: Ages 10-18 (court-involved youth)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:6 to 1:10 (24/7)
- **Regulations**: State juvenile justice, education, health departments
- **Key Features**: Incident tracking, behavior management, education tracking, court coordination
- **Licensing**: Juvenile detention facility license
- **Average Size**: 20-100 youth

#### 5.2 Halfway Houses (Adult Reentry)
- **Participants**: Adults 18+ transitioning from prison
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:10 to 1:15
- **Regulations**: State corrections, parole board
- **Key Features**: Curfew tracking, employment verification, drug testing, parole officer coordination
- **Licensing**: Halfway house license
- **Average Size**: 20-50 residents

#### 5.3 Electronic Monitoring Programs
- **Participants**: Adults 18+ on home detention
- **Staff Ratios**: Case managers, not 24/7
- **Regulations**: State corrections, courts
- **Key Features**: GPS tracking integration, schedule management, compliance reporting
- **Licensing**: Electronic monitoring program certification
- **Average Size**: 50-500 participants

#### 5.4 Day Reporting Centers
- **Participants**: Adults 18+ on probation/parole
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:20 to 1:30
- **Regulations**: State corrections, courts
- **Key Features**: Check-in tracking, programming attendance, drug testing, employment support
- **Licensing**: Day reporting center license
- **Average Size**: 100-300 participants

---

## 6. Adult Day Programs

**Primary Characteristics**:
- Daytime only (no overnight)
- Support services for vulnerable adults
- Social services coordination
- Health monitoring
- Typically state-funded or subsidized

### Subcategories

#### 6.1 Senior Day Centers
- **Participants**: Seniors 60+ needing daytime supervision
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:6 to 1:10
- **Regulations**: State aging department
- **Key Features**: Activity scheduling, health monitoring, meal tracking, transportation coordination
- **Licensing**: Adult day services license
- **Average Size**: 20-60 seniors

#### 6.2 Disability Day Services (IDD/DD)
- **Participants**: Adults 18+ with intellectual/developmental disabilities
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:3 to 1:8 depending on needs
- **Regulations**: State developmental disabilities services
- **Key Features**: Individual service plans, behavior tracking, skill development, community integration
- **Licensing**: Developmental disabilities day program license
- **Average Size**: 15-50 participants

#### 6.3 Adult Day Health Care
- **Participants**: Adults 18+ with medical needs
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:6 (includes nursing staff)
- **Regulations**: State health department
- **Key Features**: Medication administration, vital signs monitoring, therapy services
- **Licensing**: Adult day health care license
- **Average Size**: 20-40 participants

#### 6.4 Vocational Day Programs
- **Participants**: Adults with disabilities learning job skills
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:6 to 1:10
- **Regulations**: State rehabilitation services
- **Key Features**: Job coaching, skill assessments, production tracking
- **Licensing**: Vocational rehabilitation program certification
- **Average Size**: 20-60 participants

---

## 7. Treatment & Recovery Centers

**Primary Characteristics**:
- Therapeutic intervention focus
- Medical and behavioral health integration
- Program completion tracking
- Insurance billing complexity
- State substance abuse/mental health authority oversight

### Subcategories

#### 7.1 Substance Abuse Treatment (Residential)
- **Participants**: Adults 18+ (sometimes adolescents)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:6 to 1:10 (24/7)
- **Regulations**: State substance abuse authority, HIPAA
- **Key Features**: Treatment plans, group therapy tracking, drug testing, discharge planning
- **Licensing**: Residential substance abuse treatment license
- **Average Size**: 20-60 residents

#### 7.2 Substance Abuse Treatment (Outpatient/IOP)
- **Participants**: Adults 18+ (and adolescents)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:12 to 1:15 for groups
- **Regulations**: State substance abuse authority, HIPAA
- **Key Features**: Program attendance, drug testing, individual/group sessions
- **Licensing**: Outpatient treatment license
- **Average Size**: 50-200 clients

#### 7.3 Eating Disorder Treatment Centers
- **Participants**: Adolescents and adults (typically females 12-40)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:4 to 1:6 (residential), 1:8 (outpatient)
- **Regulations**: State health/mental health departments, HIPAA
- **Key Features**: Meal monitoring, weight tracking, therapy sessions, medical monitoring
- **Licensing**: Mental health residential treatment facility license
- **Average Size**: 15-40 residents (residential), 30-100 (outpatient)

#### 7.4 Behavioral Health Day Treatment
- **Participants**: Children, adolescents, or adults with mental health needs
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:6 to 1:10
- **Regulations**: State mental health authority, HIPAA
- **Key Features**: Intensive programming, skills groups, individual therapy, crisis management
- **Licensing**: Partial hospitalization/day treatment license
- **Average Size**: 30-80 participants

#### 7.5 Autism Treatment Centers (ABA)
- **Participants**: Children ages 2-18 with autism
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:1 to 1:3 (ABA therapy)
- **Regulations**: State health/education departments, insurance requirements, HIPAA
- **Key Features**: Behavior data collection, treatment plans, parent training, progress graphing
- **Licensing**: Behavioral health services license
- **Average Size**: 30-100 clients

---

## 8. Community & Recreation Centers

**Primary Characteristics**:
- Community enrichment focus
- Drop-in and scheduled programming
- Membership-based or fee-for-service
- Lighter regulatory burden (typically)
- Focus on activities and social engagement

### Subcategories

#### 8.1 Youth Recreation Centers
- **Participants**: Ages 5-18
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:15 to 1:25
- **Regulations**: Local permits, liability insurance, background checks
- **Key Features**: Activity registration, membership management, attendance tracking
- **Licensing**: Business license, recreational facility permit
- **Average Size**: 100-500 youth members

#### 8.2 Senior Centers
- **Participants**: Adults 55+
- **Staff Ratios**: Varies (not strictly regulated)
- **Regulations**: Minimal (local permits)
- **Key Features**: Activity calendar, meal services, transportation coordination
- **Licensing**: Business license
- **Average Size**: 200-1000 members

#### 8.3 Community Centers (General)
- **Participants**: All ages
- **Staff Ratios**: Varies by program
- **Regulations**: Local permits
- **Key Features**: Facility reservations, program registration, membership management
- **Licensing**: Business license
- **Average Size**: 500-5000 members

#### 8.4 Sports & Fitness Training Facilities
- **Participants**: All ages (often specialized by sport)
- **Staff Ratios**: 1:10 to 1:20 for group training
- **Regulations**: Liability insurance, certifications for coaches
- **Key Features**: Team management, practice schedules, performance tracking
- **Licensing**: Business license
- **Average Size**: 100-500 athletes

## Platform Considerations by Institution Type

### Regulatory Compliance Complexity

**High Complexity** (Most regulatory oversight):
- Healthcare facilities (HIPAA, medical licensing)
- Residential care facilities (24/7 care requirements)
- Correctional facilities (justice system coordination)
- Infant/toddler centers (strict ratios and requirements)

**Medium Complexity**:
- K-12 schools (education regulations)
- Treatment centers (clinical documentation)
- Adult day programs (state funding requirements)

**Lower Complexity**:
- Tutoring centers
- Recreation centers
- Some specialty schools

### Data Requirements

**Most Complex Data Models**:
- Healthcare facilities (medical records, HIPAA)
- Treatment centers (clinical assessments, progress notes)
- Residential facilities (24/7 logs, incident tracking)

**Standard Data Models**:
- Educational institutions (academic records)
- Child care centers (daily reports)
- Day programs (attendance, activities)

**Simpler Data Models**:
- Recreation centers (membership, registration)
- Drop-in programs

### Staff Ratio Enforcement

**Strict Real-Time Enforcement Needed**:
- Child care centers (regulatory compliance)
- Residential facilities (safety requirements)
- Healthcare facilities (quality of care)

**Scheduling-Based Enforcement**:
- Educational institutions
- Treatment centers
- Day programs

**Flexible Ratios**:
- Recreation centers
- Community programs

## Pricing Considerations by Type

### Subscription Tiers

**Starter ($99/month)**:
- Simpler institution types
- Lower participant counts (<50)
- Examples: Tutoring centers, small preschools, recreation programs

**Professional ($299/month)**:
- Moderate complexity
- Mid-size operations (50-150 participants)
- Examples: Full-day child care, small schools, outpatient clinics

**Enterprise ($999/month)**:
- High complexity or large scale
- Residential facilities, high regulatory burden
- Examples: Assisted living, residential treatment, hospitals, large schools
