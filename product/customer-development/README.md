## Best Practices

### Conducting Problem Interviews

**Do**:
- ✅ Ask about past behavior ("Tell me about the last time...")
- ✅ Listen more than talk (80/20 rule)
- ✅ Dig deep with "Why?" questions
- ✅ Take detailed notes
- ✅ Look for patterns across interviews

**Don't**:
- ❌ Pitch your solution
- ❌ Ask leading questions
- ❌ Ask hypothetical questions ("Would you use...")
- ❌ Ignore negative feedback
- ❌ Cherry-pick responses that validate your bias

---

### Interview Tips

**Opening**: "I'm learning about how people [context]. I'm not selling anything today, just trying to understand your experience."

**Good Questions**:
- "Tell me about the last time you felt unsafe walking..."
- "How did you solve that problem?"
- "What's frustrating about current solutions?"
- "How much time/money does that cost you?"

**Bad Questions**:
- "Would you use an app that..." (hypothetical)
- "Do you think this is a good idea?" (seeking validation)
- "Don't you hate it when..." (leading)
