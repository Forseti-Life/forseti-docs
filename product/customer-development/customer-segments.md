## Overview

Customer segmentation divides the market into distinct groups of people who have different needs, characteristics, or behaviors. This document defines our target customer segments and detailed personas.

## Market Segmentation Framework

### Segmentation Criteria

1. **Geographic**: Where do they live?
2. **Demographic**: Age, income, education, family status
3. **Psychographic**: Values, attitudes, lifestyle
4. **Behavioral**: How they interact with safety/technology

### Persona 1: "Sarah the Safe Commuter"

![Persona Image Placeholder]

#### Background
- **Age**: 32
- **Occupation**: Marketing Manager
- **Location**: Chicago, IL
- **Living Situation**: Rents 1BR apartment in Lincoln Park
- **Income**: $75,000/year

#### Daily Life
- Walks 15 minutes to/from Red Line station daily
- Works 9am-6pm, sometimes late hours
- Socializes with friends on weekends (bars, restaurants)
- Runs outdoors 3x per week

#### Goals & Motivations
- Stay safe during daily commutes
- Be aware of risks in new neighborhoods
- Make informed decisions about routes/timing
- Feel confident exploring the city

#### Frustrations & Pain Points
- General crime stats don't help with specific routes
- Doesn't know if an area is safe until it's too late
- Friends share anecdotes but not reliable data
- Uncomfortable in unfamiliar areas after dark

#### Technology Profile
- iPhone user, downloads 2-3 apps per month
- Uses Google Maps daily, Waze occasionally
- Follows safety/neighborhood groups on Reddit, Nextdoor
- Willing to pay $5-10/month for valuable services

#### How They Find Solutions
- App Store search for "safety" or "crime"
- Reddit recommendations (r/chicago)
- Word of mouth from friends/coworkers
- Local news articles about safety

#### Buying Criteria
- **Must Have**: Real-time, accurate data
- **Nice to Have**: Background monitoring, alerts
- **Deal Breakers**: Inaccurate data, poor UX, high cost

#### Quote
> "I just want to know if I should take a different route home. Is that too much to ask?"

## Early Adopters

**Who will be our first 100 customers?**

Early adopters have these characteristics:
1. **Have the problem** (they experience it acutely)
2. **Know they have the problem** (they're actively looking for solutions)
3. **Have been looking for a solution** (they've tried alternatives)
4. **Have assembled a solution themselves** (DIY, workarounds)
5. **Have or can acquire a budget** (willing and able to pay)

## Customer Journey Map

### Awareness Stage
_How do they first learn about the problem?_

**Triggers**:
- Personal experience (witnessed crime, felt unsafe)
- News story about local crime
- Friend's story or recommendation
- Moving to new city/neighborhood

**Actions**:
- Google search for "[city] crime map"
- Ask friends/coworkers about neighborhood safety
- Check city police website
- Read local subreddit

### Consideration Stage
_How do they evaluate solutions?_

**Actions**:
- Download 2-3 safety apps to compare
- Read app reviews
- Check privacy policies
- Test free features before paying

**Decision Criteria**:
- Accuracy and freshness of data
- User interface and ease of use
- Price and value
- Privacy and security practices

### Purchase Stage
_What makes them convert?_

**Actions**:
- Start free trial or use freemium version
- Experience "aha moment" (alert saves them from danger)
- Compare to alternatives
- Decide to subscribe

**Conversion Factors**:
- Strong first experience (activation)
- Clear value proposition
- Reasonable price
- Trust in brand/data

### Retention Stage
_What keeps them coming back?_

**Actions**:
- Check app before walking in new areas
- Receive and act on alerts
- Share with friends/family
- Adjust settings based on needs

**Retention Factors**:
- Consistent value delivery (useful alerts)
- Habit formation (check before going out)
- Data quality (trust in information)
- Feature improvements
