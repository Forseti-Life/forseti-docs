## Purpose

This directory contains the Lean Canvas for Forseti products - a 1-page business model that helps identify the riskiest parts of our business model and prioritize what to test first.

The Lean Canvas is adapted from the Business Model Canvas but optimized for startups and rapid iteration.

## What is a Lean Canvas?

The Lean Canvas is designed to be:
- **Fast**: Complete in 15-20 minutes
- **Concise**: Fits on one page
- **Portable**: Share easily with team and advisors
- **Iterative**: Update frequently as you learn

### Key Differences from Business Model Canvas

| Business Model Canvas | Lean Canvas |
|----------------------|-------------|
| Solution | **Problem** (focus on customer pain) |
| Key Partners | **Problem** (understand before partnering) |
| Key Activities | **Key Metrics** (measure what matters) |
| Key Resources | **Unfair Advantage** (what can't be copied?) |
| Customer Relationships | **Channels** (simpler, startup-focused) |

## Order of Completion

The Lean Canvas should be filled out in this specific order to maintain focus on learning:

### 1. Problem (Start Here)
Identify the top 3 problems your customers have. Understanding the problem is more important than having a solution.

**Questions to Answer**:
- What are the top 3 problems customers face?
- How painful are these problems? (1-10 scale)
- What do customers use today to solve these problems?

---

### 2. Customer Segments
Who has these problems most acutely?

**Questions to Answer**:
- Who are your target customers?
- Who are your early adopters? (most desperate for solution)
- What characteristics define them?

---

### 3. Unique Value Proposition (UVP)
Single, clear, compelling message that states why you're different and worth attention.

**Formula**: [End result customer wants] + [Specific period of time] + [Address objections]

**Questions to Answer**:
- What is the single most compelling benefit?
- Why is it unique/different from alternatives?
- Can you say it in one clear sentence?

---

### 4. Solution
Top 3 features that address the top 3 problems.

**Questions to Answer**:
- What's the minimum feature set to solve the problem?
- Which features directly address each problem?
- What can you cut while still delivering value?

---

### 5. Channels
How will you reach customers?

**Questions to Answer**:
- Where do your customers spend time?
- What's your path to customers? (free vs. paid)
- Which channels will you test first?

---

### 6. Revenue Streams
How will you make money?

**Questions to Answer**:
- What will customers pay for?
- How much will they pay? (pricing model)
- What's your revenue model? (subscription, one-time, freemium)

---

### 7. Cost Structure
What are your major costs?

**Questions to Answer**:
- What are your fixed costs? (salaries, infrastructure)
- What are your variable costs? (per customer)
- What's your burn rate?

---

### 8. Key Metrics
What numbers will you measure?

**Questions to Answer**:
- What's your North Star Metric?
- What are your AARRR metrics? (Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral)
- What metrics indicate product-market fit?

---

### 9. Unfair Advantage (Fill Last)
What makes you hard to copy?

**Note**: It's okay to leave this blank initially. Real unfair advantages are built over time.

**Questions to Answer**:
- What do you have that can't be easily copied or bought?
- Examples: Network effects, exclusive partnerships, proprietary data, insider information

## Using the Lean Canvas

### 1. Start with Draft
Fill out canvas based on current assumptions (15-20 minutes).

### 2. Identify Risks
Highlight boxes that are most uncertain or risky.

**Risk Priority**:
- 🔴 **High Risk**: Total guess, no validation
- 🟡 **Medium Risk**: Some evidence, needs more validation
- 🟢 **Low Risk**: Well validated through customer feedback

### 3. Test Riskiest Parts First
Create experiments to validate riskiest assumptions.

**Example**: 
- If "Problem" is 🔴 High Risk → Run problem validation interviews
- If "Revenue Streams" is 🔴 High Risk → Test pricing with surveys/trials

### 4. Update Frequently
Canvas should evolve as you learn.

**Update Triggers**:
- After completing customer interviews
- After experiment results
- After major pivot
- Monthly review (minimum)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Spending Too Much Time
**Problem**: Trying to make it "perfect"  
**Solution**: 15-20 minutes for first draft, iterate later

### ❌ Focusing on Solution First
**Problem**: Starting with "what we'll build"  
**Solution**: Start with problem, understand customer pain first

### ❌ Making It Static
**Problem**: Create once and never update  
**Solution**: Treat as living document, update after learning

### ❌ Lack of Specificity
**Problem**: Vague statements like "everyone" or "mobile users"  
**Solution**: Be specific about customer segments and problems

### ❌ Ignoring Existing Alternatives
**Problem**: Assuming no competition  
**Solution**: Document what customers use today (even if imperfect)
